Dear Diary,

Xulleyl calls us her "Spider Guard" and her first command was that we all turn on each other for her amusement. She called it a "test" but what did it test other than martial skill? I found this "test" to be a mark against her competence to be honest, because martial skill is no testament to leadership and other skills may be powerful and even overwhelming but they do not shine in the kind of free-for-all she orchestrated. Given time, some of those who fell first may become the most powerful of the Spider Guard. I found her game offensive.

Now I will describe my companions, as I suspect my fate is tied to them for some time to come.

After witnessing the brutal massacre of the caravan that brought me to Nerr'Tenden I was blindfolded and taken to a barracks where I was unceremoniously dumped off and left to meet for the first time my new companions. I was shaken and panicked and not at my best.

Valron immediately caught my attention. A large, imposing elf with a wicked-looking trident always by his side. He is an elf I would not dare to cross in battle, and he seems the type who is true to his companions above all. However, there is something sinister about him: he carries a number of vials and constantly sprinkles the contents in our food. I find this objectionable to say the least but I will not elaborate further, lest someone discover this diary. I'll just say this: he may be the one I'm looking out for but to be honest I hope he is not, as I believe Valron may be a loyal and forthright ally.

I just need to clear up this business with the vials.

Inarra is an imposing self-possessed beauty who clearly thinks the world is solely hers to manipulate. She may even be right but I hope she will contain herself. I can see that she could wield undue influence over me without me realizing it. Her presence and ... and breathtaking beauty are hard to ... resist. She's capable of anything. She's .... well, even the others realize the danger of letting her influence them so she may have the hardest time integrating into the group. It's so obvious she sees us as things to manipulate rather than as companions.

Zaxch is another who clearly displays loyalty to those he has chosen to be his companions. He and Valron are already inseparable, which I consider to be a point in Valron's favor. Like me, Zaxch was a slave but while he knows nothing of me I remember him clearly. Even then he was a formidable pit fighter admired by all the slaves, but after witnessing his prowess up close in the free-for-all I can only say that I hope to always be on his good side. He appears to be the most fit to direct our battlefield operations and I will gladly follow him into battle against any foe. One thing I must note. He stands no chance to remain as leader. The women are simply too ambitious to allow it. But he has already earned my loyalty as a companion in the Spider Guard.

Which brings me to Kadyel.

She is formidable and bossy. Yet she inspires confidence. I willingly offered her the leadership role during the free-for-all in exchange for helping me defeat the others. That was yet another failure on my part, as I couldn't even remain standing long enough for her to join me.

It is obvious that she is one of the most capable and self-sufficient of the group. I only fear that there will be some clash between her and Inarra or between her and Zaxch. Her ambition fills the room the moment she walks in and among this group, that may lead to problems. I only hope that others can see that she is the most suited to leadership.

Sorn displays the grace and beauty of a lifelong dancer. His every move betrays a trained eloquence of movement that is compelling and delightful to see. He was not built for combat yet he possesses an alien, eldritch power I do not understand. During the free-for-all, he refrained from using that power, choosing to attempt less dangerous barehanded strikes instead. He clearly had no chance to win using this tactic yet he did it anyway. Thus, I believe he is also the kind who chooses loyalty over glory, which I admire. He seems to hold a healthy respect for the females in charge, which is wise and appropriate.

Nal. Nal. What kind of elf is he? I don't know what to say of him at this point. We have exchanged few words yet that makes me the one he has spoken to the most. It is not because of him that I choose to sleep alone in the common room, but his intimidating and unsettling presence certainly doesn't make it a difficult choice. Sometimes when he looks at me, it feels like he is examining a bug in a jar. To call him "unsettling" may be a tremendous understatement.

I suspect he harbors a quiet and dangerous ambition that surpasses even Kadyel's naked desire for power. I have some hope that we can be allies but he is difficult to read at this point. I only pray to Lolth he never finds me helpless and unable to move ...

Len We is mystery to me. He is clearly trained in the ways of Lolth's Fang but there is more to him. He has revealed almost nothing about himself and he remains quiet and aloof.

Signing off for now, May Her Web Protect Me,

Trynn
