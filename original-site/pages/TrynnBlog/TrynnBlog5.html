TrynnBlog/TrynnBlog5

My parents were destroyed by House Oussund during a purge of traitors.

They tried to take us in our sleep, and but for a loyal friend who came to warn us of the coming violence, they would have succeeded. A quick, raspy scratch outside our residence. A scrape of stone upon stone. Two scrapes. Pause. Two scrapes more.

My father leaped to his feat in an instant, as if he had been waiting for that sound.

He trembled visibly, whether from adrenaline or fear I could not tell. But he whispered fiercely to my mother as quiet footsteps retreated from our door. "We are betrayed!"

Those words have haunted me ever since, and I am still to be convinced that he was really a traitor to House Oussund. He was betrayed. Why else would he say as much when there was no one of influence to hear it?

And there is one other piece of evidence in his favor. It is said that blood runs true, and the sins and virtues of the parents are passed on to the children. But if blood runs true then my parents were no traitors, for I am loyal to House Oussund.

That dark day of the purge, my parents were luckier than some others. They got a warning from a loyal friend at least.

I remember my father hissing desperately at us to "Go!" and then turning to face the intruders we could already hear approaching, his rapier held ready.

Unlike the clerics and nobles of the highborn, my parents held genuine affection for each other. I will never forget how they paused, how she squeezed his hand briefly before whisking me out and into the secret escape route they had planned. I've never seen a highborn so much as smile at a male but my mother stopped and burned precious seconds to squeeze his hand, knowing he was doomed.

We ran.

As we fled I could hear the sounds of battle, strange sounds to my young ears. The sounds of metal biting into flesh, of blood splattering a floor, the sounds of my father dying in his attempt to give us precious minutes for our escape.

...

We survived for weeks. First we hid outside of Dolthgurdar. My parents had secured hidden supplies for just this occasion. We moved each day, recovering food and water from secret caches out in rarely travelled tunnels. My mother spoke to me of House intrigues, of factions and of religion, of things like prestige and jealousy and power. I was too young to understand it all but I understood the lessons behind it. I learned that the House was not to be crossed. It was enough to even be mistaken for an enemy. House Oussand would find you and crush you.

Eventually we returned in secret to her cousin's house. The search was still on and she knew that she couldn't hide forever. She arranged a deal to give up in return for sparing me and they accepted. She was indentured for a hundred years and I was made a slave of the clerics. It was a marvelously successful outcome for us compared to what they had intended of course. All thanks to my father, who sacrificed himself to give us a brief moment to run.

I remember that she squeezed my hand as they took me away forever. She told me, "this is your new mother now," but I knew she didn't mean it. It could never be true. Even my loyalty to House Oussund could waver if she asked it. But she didn't ask it, and that is but more evidence to me that she was not a traitor. Since then I have seen her only a handful of times. Secret meetings arranged for short moments in time, a risk worth taking.

I was assigned as the personal slave of a young cleric. She took on the role of a mother, only very different from my own. Domineering, harsh and unforgiving. I was whipped when I did wrong, rewarded when I groveled. She taught me what I needed to learn. I am thankful to her for that.

....

I later learned they had executed my father, then revived his corpse to work as a mindless automaton scout in the territories threatened by psionic races. I do not blame the Matriarch for this. She acted on what information she had. It is only natural to crush your enemies while you still can, and to make future enemies think twice about the consequences they will face.

....

Over the years it became apparent I was gifted with unusual strength and agility, so my "mother" had me trained to dance for their ceremonies and gatherings.

I loved it. It was a release, an escape from my life as a slave. When I danced, I was free to move how I wanted, to be my own agent. Every waking moment of my day and night, every action every thought, were under complete control of my cleric "mother" except for when I danced. My dancing was very good. She rewarded me with lighter duties; I was not required to work the kitchens anymore. But she was still liberal with the lash when it was warranted.

It was arranged for me to be trained as a bodyguard, and so they sent me to an old retired guard with bad eyesight name Ghuan'Diirn, now living in peace near the outskirts of Dolthgurdar. The best part of my training was the long walks from the slave quarters to his residence. It was a kind of freedom, wandering the streets, witnessing the normal lives of Dolthgurdar. The clerics had no fear of me running away. They could find me with ease, and we all knew that my indentured mother would be punished for any of my failures. What they didn't know was that my loyalty to her transcended even my fear of them. I was able to arrange secret meetings with her more frequently. They were brief and innocent, usually just a quiet moment over tea while she asked me about my life with a mother's concern.

If the best part of my day was walking to meet with Ghuan, the hardest part of my day was walking home. The training left me beaten and exhausted. His training was brutal, but not cruel. He was demanding, but not vindictive. He may have been old, but he was certainly not weak.

My training was as much mental as physical. He trained me to endure, to suffer, to have a mental discipline that would serve me for any challenge.

We spent hours balancing with heavy weights, sparring upside down suspended from ropes, running courses blindfolded, fighting with hands numbed from ice, boxing barefoot on a floor strewn with sharp rocks, and more. Every day was a new pain. I lived with the pain. Every strike, every hold, every excercise was agony. I limped home every day. I resented much of it at the time, but it made me strong.

And he was able to spot my latency when no one else had. He taught me to look into myself, to dig deep from my own psychic will when I felt I had nothing left. I didn't quite understand what he meant but I tried. I could feel it tantalizingly near, a oneness of body and mind that would surely lend me strength.

He warned me not to tell the clerics of all we practiced. I found this odd, and scary, but I did as he instructed. As far as they were concerned, an old decrepit monk was teaching me some basics, that is all.

And then came the day that I died.

...

The priestess came into my quarters without knocking or even looking at me. At first, I thought for sure my life was forfeit, that they had determined some petty transgression and were sending me to the pits, which would have been death for me.

She was reading something in her hands and paused just long enough to snap at me. "You will accompany me as the Second. Now." And she left. She never even looked up. Her name was Shaille, but I only knew that because of overhearing conversations amongst the other slaves.

So, I was to be given my first assignment as a bodyguard to Lolth's chosen. Nearly an honor. Mostly just an opportunity to be punished for failure. That much I understood. I secured my weapons and met them out front, the cleric with her First. He eyed me with disdain and dislike. The bodyguards are constantly shifted around in rank and privilege, causing jealousy and intense competition. The clerics like it that way of course. They say it brings out the best.

"We are to meet at a rendezvous point where I will confer with someone. You of course shall remember nothing if asked. That is your role. Watch, protect, sacrifice." And she turned on her heel. The First walked in front, and I in back. She led us out of Dolthgurdar, into caves and tunnels.

I was jumpy and scared. I worried that my nerves would betray, and I focused on my inner will, as Ghuan had taught me.

They struck quickly and the cleric was down before I could even react. I saw the First draw his sword and then darkness descended as I fumbled with my whip. Damn! Then a loud cracking clap of sound nearly deafened me followed by a gurgling gasp from the First. I had no idea what to do. I turned to retreat and at least get out of the globe of darkness. That is when something struck my head and I was out, the smell of ozone the last thing I remembered.

...

I awoke slowly. Someone was dragging me along the floor, holding my armpits and pulling me from behind. Between my two feet, I could see three figures lying on the floor. The cleric, and the First, and another figure, all charred beyond recognition. Something odd..... something not quite right... the third figure was dressed and outfitted just like me! The same gear, and the same build, everything. A charred husk. Was I dead?

"He wakes". A figure crouched in my view, putting a small vial in my hand. "They will come to clean up. Stay quiet, they will not find you. Drink this when you are ready to move again." Then something heavy was slipped into my pocket. "Read this when you wake. Your masters will think you dead, so be wary". I drifted off, agony pulsing across my skull. My masters would think me dead, and so would Mother. I wished she could squeeze my hand one more time.

I finally woke again to the sounds of angry voices as they cleaned up the bodies and searched for clues. I had been stuffed into a crevasse down a side passage. I was cramped and hurt and thirsty, but alive. I waited in discomfort until they left.

I now make my way to the Crystal Gate, as the note instructed. What choice do I have? I have failed the clerics, my first assignment an utter failure that left a priestess dead. if they discover me alive they will be forced to assume I am a traitor.

And who is behind this latest turn for me? Or is it the clerics testing my loyalty? Or someone else entirely? My mother perhaps? I have no choice but to see where this goes. I can trust no one...
