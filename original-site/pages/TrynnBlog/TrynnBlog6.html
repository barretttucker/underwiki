TrynnBlog/TrynnBlog6

I can only hope that my miserable display of ineptitude in the mines will go unpunished by Xulleyl. I pray she will give me another chance to show my worth.

We entered with confidence. We felt cocky I think, too cocky. It became immediately apparent that they were ready for us. They knew we were coming. Perhaps they were warned by some traitor among us in the barracks.

Nevertheless we started well. Despite my clumsy use of the innate darkness, we succeeded is pushing them back into the next turn of the cave. But that is where things went wrong. It was my fault yet again.

The mage blinded us all because I was too slow to realize the setup. I failed Xulleyl and I failed the Spiderguard. I should have understood that the mirror was setup for a blinding spell. Curse my slow wit. By Lolth's slimy tongue I will not let it happen again.

But fate saved us from my mistake in a way, although I am not convinced this newcomer is altogether a good thing for us. *Something* arrived and eliminated the remaining force, mage and all. There was nothing left. THE VERY WALLS HAD MELTED UNDER THE FIRES! It was the last straw. I wanted only to leave those mines and flee whatever demon was stalking us.

But Kadyel was right, we had to finish our mission. Kadeyl... ah Kadeyl. She seemed so well-suited to a role of leadership... at first. We'll see if she learns from her mistakes because even I can see she may lack the prudence to lead us in times of high stress and danger. Elves can die in the situations we find ourselves and and playing recklessly with our lives is not good leadership. But who else can lead us? It's strange to think of being led by a male. It must be Kadyel. Who else is truly suited?

Nal. Perhaps. He is not like any elf I have ever met, but his sheer force of personality cannot be ignored. Perhaps, even though he is male, he could very well act as a leader. I would have to be more convinced. I do respect his direct and forthright critiques. It feels honest rather than vindictive and I am fine with that. Afterall, I did make mistakes.

Inarra? No. Everything and everyone is a tool to her, even her own self! Yes, she is a tool to herself which is a bizarre concept I have just discovered. She is more suited to ... other roles.

Yet... no. Nevermind. Not Innarra.

Kadyel. I would like to think she will live up to her potential but only time will tell.

If I had failed a little less spectacularly myself I may have the courage to speak with her. Perhaps next time, if I don't fall on my face again in front of the others.

By Lolth's pointed tongue.

The Loyal Phoenix...
