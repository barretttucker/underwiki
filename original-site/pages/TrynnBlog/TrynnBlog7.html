The assassination

So, Xulleyl has turned us into assassins now.

When we joined up with the Keniryan contingent they already mistrusted us. So much so that we marched and camped separately at their command. We were to give a "gift" to Ilium. But of course it wasn't really a gift. I'm surprised the others took so long to realize.

At first they were suprised and a little confused after opening the box. But I knew what we had to do. It was obvious. I said as much and the plan took shape. Xulleyl had turned us into assassins.

But to what purpose? Why kill a man who is only going to be raised in a couple days? It's obviously some kind of message. But Xulleyl's machinations are opaque to me at this time. Apparently she felt it would accomplish something valuable and that is enough.

But it didn't turn out so well. I feel that once again we failed Xulleyl.

It started with a botched attempt at "delivery". In retrospect there's probably little we could have done. Keniryan was so paranoid and distrustful that no matter what they would have assumed it was us. Inarra was nearly killed and barely managed to get out of range of Selbs and Yaz.

At the beginning of combat I happened to be near the back with Kadyel. We heard troops approaching from behind down a narrow passage. We both decided to head them off, bottling them up. If they got through into the open who knows what they could have done. Zacxh yelled something to us as we headed down the passage but it wasn't clear. Something about "get back here". It wasn't until later that he made it *VERY* clear I had disobeyed direct orders. I pledged not to let that happen again.

Zacxh showed his disappointment by striking me across the face. The poor soul only knows the language of violence. I don't hold it against him. It bothers me to see him still so warped by his past, but hopefully he can grow past it.

Kadyel and I somehow managed to hold them all off. We took some injury but held strong. Finally they routed and as we returned to the main cavern, Keniyrin launched their attack on us. There was no room for negotiation. They wanted us dead and who can blame them? They were certain we had killed Ilium and of course they were right. I don't fault them their ferocity. They presented a formidable challenge even with Zacxh and Kadyel at the front line.

It was my inner mental power of expansion that got us the victory. I'm proud of that. Zacxh had a tattoo I'd given him. We both presented an impressive front. Some of the Keniryan minions simply fled from us in fear. But Yaz and Selbs may have prevailed alone. They were absolutely furious and better fighters than I've ever faced. Better than Zacxh even.

But the scariest moment for me came from our own side in the form of Nal. We were hard pressed, fighting ferociously, when suddenly Nal leaped between Zacxh and I and with a loud curse in an unintelligible language he lightly touched Yaz on the forehead, nothing more.

For a silent, eerie moment both stood still like two dancers frozen in time, and then Yaz slipped quietly to the ground. She was dead.

I nearly forgot to continue fighting Selbs as I stared at Nal in wonder and awe. Even Selbs was affected I believe because after that her fighting was lackluster, as if too shocked to really focus anymore. Nal melted back into the rearguard and was silent as we mopped up the remaining Keniryan resistance.

I will never forget that moment.

My Lolth's web protect us...
