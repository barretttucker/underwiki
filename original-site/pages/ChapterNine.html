Chapter Nine: Basking in the afterglow

Zacxh finished his bath quickly. Certainly The meeting between Xulleyl and Valron’s aunt would conclude quickly and Zacxh did not want to upset his captain by being absent when called to duty. He toweled off quickly and began to dress when he realized how badly damaged his fine silk shirt and uniform cloak was from the fire. Zacxh borrowed a brush from the bath master and used it to scrub out and sanitize his beautiful leather armor. After a few minutes of work, the finely wrought stuff began to shine. He discarded his ruined shirt and cloak and dressed minimally in only buffed leather for his return to the barracks. A tip to the bath attendant and he was on his way, much refreshed.

Something seemed missing as Zacxh trekked through the courtyard. It took only a moment to realize that the screaming had stopped. Zacxh looked to see Anthadax absently examine the bodies of two mages and then wander off in his creepy disinterested way.

Zacxh moved quickly. He grabbed up the two bodies of the mages trying hard to appear as part of the general working party straightening up after the battle. He hauled the dead weight bodies effortlessly around a corner and into a discreet location to strip them of valuables. He found some interesting possibly magical trinkets for Sorn and a beautiful dagger for himself and was about to abandon the corpses when he realized one was not actually dead.

Anthadax had not bothered to kill this one, none the less his destruction of the elf was complete. Zacxh looked into the elf’s eyes. There was nothing to be found there. Zacxh knew the symptom well. In his time as a slave occasionally an elf would receive the 'gift' of mercy after having endured torture beyond bearing. The mind was shattered and even if sensibility was restored, the elf would become a shattered broken soul, or worse a nightmare tortured monster hell bent on a mix of revenge and self destruction.

Zacxh examined the trappings of this one. A picture of this elf emerged from his possessions. He was a mage of some promise but still young in his art. He might have one day become something worthwhile, but now, with this deep psychological wound... This poor soul was lost.

Zacxh imagined two possibilities. He saw in his mind the mage mentally crippled, carrying water from the Orlylyl well to the amusement of sneering warriors. The other possibility was much worse. He envisioned him twisted and obscene, pouring over the light of a dim candle researching twisted arcana in an ultimately fruitless quest for power and revenge. never again would he allow himself to fall victim to one more powerful. Never would he allow himself to be so shamed.

Either way this elf was destroyed. Anthadax knew his powers well.

Zacxh leaned in and administered one last test.

'Elf my name Zacxh and I am going to kill you unless you ask otherwise.'

Nothing. The eyes stared thoughtlessly ahead from a sagging face.

Zacxh sighed and put his hand over the mages nose and mouth. As the mage began to turn blue, Zacxh remembered something he had overheard someone once say about not wanting to be sustained if her were wounded in battle in such a way as to become simple.

Normally, there would be some struggle as the elf’s subconscious survival mechanism kicked in to preserve what was left. Even that was destroyed or subverted by the torture. The mage died without a twitch.

Zacxh waited a moment lost in thought , then picked up the bodies and brought them to the front gates. He brushed past Ardul's guards and strode unconcerned to the corporal of the Orlylyl troop waiting outside.

'I believe these are yours. I apologize for having detained them while they were interrogated by our mage. Though the 'discussion' went on for awhile, I do not believe that they really had much to say.'

There was a slight chuckle from one of Ardul's troop. Zacxh was pleased that for once, his humor had found an appreciative ear. He spun on his heel and returned to the Spiderguard barracks.

Zacxh changed into one of his fine black silk shirts and began to work on maintaining his weapons and gear. He worked ritualistically, shining and oiling and polishing everything to a new and gleaming sheen of oil and lightly fragranced sweat. Finally satisfied, he turned his attention to the sack of gear he had salvaged on the battlefield. He was just beginning the task of sorting it into the growing inventory arrayed about the main chamber of the Spiderguard barracks when a knock came at the chamber door. Everyone in the room immediately recognized it as the knock of Captain Xulleyl. Chairs scraped and bodies shuffled to attention as the door opened. Nal seemed to quickly conceal something he was working on as the chamber door flung open.

The Captain, Steward, and Orlylyl priestess marched in. Captain Xulleyl was about to speak but the Priestess rudely blurted in a voice just short of rage.

'I Have discussed the situation with your Captain here and we have decided to investigate this matter fully.

Before I go, I want to see each of you clearly. We *will* meet again... so stand and state your name that I may mark you.'

Each Spiderguard stood and stated their name around the room, until the priestess's gaze fell upon Zacxh.

'They call me The Knife priestess.' Zacxh cared little for her petty display of power.

She Seethed at the unsatisfactory answer.

'Well I refuse to call you that. What is your name slave?'

Zacxh decided to save this fight for another day.

'Zacxh.'

She leered a moment and continued around the room. Once her interrogation was complete, she departed. Zacxh felt a surge of resentment in her presumption. He suddenly felt ashamed that he had not defied her more resolutely, if not for his own sake, then at least to show Trynn that such nonsense should not be tolerated.

Zacxh understood the exercise well. By asserting herself such deep within the enclave of her enemy, she established a hold everyone present.

Captain Xulleyl showed her the door and turned to address the party.

'Apparently she thinks she can toss around orders here...'

'Apparently she is correct' Zacxh mumbled well under his breath.

Captain Xulleyl issued orders and solicited a few questions then departed. The guard was to resume mining operations and be available for any ops that the Captain dreamed up in the meantime.

Zacxh went to the practice arena and ran through his exercises then returned to his room to rest for the evening.

He sat down and leaned back on the cool stone of the small chamber still shared with Valron. He could feel the subtle vibrations of steam and hot water which ran throughout the deep stone of the Underdark, and knew that somewhere far above, those movements were shepherded by the motions of a huge 'flying stone' which dominated the endless openness of sky above the heads of groveling burn elves. Gnomish scholars purportedly had a simple physical answer about how and why the flight of this alleged rock caused the Underdark to rhythmically breath its titanic surges of steam, water and magma, but of course the daughters of Loth claimed that it was merely 'the will of the spider queen.' Zacxh was sure the gnomes and dwarves and burn races all had similar bogus explanations dealing with their own gods and heroes. The walls, meanwhile, breathed on, warming and cooling, belching steam and fire and throbbing with the uncanny regularity that they would doubtless continue to exhibit long after the races of drow and man and the will of Loth were nothing more than forgotten legend.

The vibrations of the flow had slowed to a near crawl, and the color of the walls here were just cooling. It must be early yet, too early to rest again, but Zacxh closed his eyes anyway and tried to blink away the memory of that poor mage screaming himself mad.

Zacxh rested throughout the ebb, and halfway through another. He came to his senses stiff and haunted by visions of Anthadax, so he grabbed a heavy and battered practice sword and spent some time panting and sweating out the herbs of the bathhouse in the practice arena. He returned to the barracks refreshed and loose.

Slowly the main barracks filled with elves, and without much fuss, the Spiderguard gathered the equipment and rations needed to camp out in the mines for a few days and made the hour long trek to the dig, accompanied of course by Nal's troop of conscripts.

Despite the fact that Zacxh had repeatedly reminded the conscripts that they answered to Valron, they defaulted in their requests for guidance to Nal. Zacxh couldn't find a real objection to that, but somehow it still bothered him. He wondered if Nal might not use them in an objectionable way, all too well remembering having to fight through the little unit to get to Kadyel. Zacxh wondered if that battle might not one day be rejoined. The three who he had battered through that day were standing nearby and Zacxh noticed that they tended not to meet his eye and give him a wide berth.

Valron and Nal took charge of the mining outpost security and Zacxh conferred with his recently selected foreman to ensure all was progressing well. Before long the three units, miners, conscripts, and the Spiderguard were all settled comfortably in. The site was secure and well protected. At Zacxh's suggestion, Nal sent a few local patrols out to ensure privacy. It seemed just a matter of time before the mineral was uncovered. Once that happened, the shit would surely hit the fan so Zacxh resolved to gain the confidence of the troops and miners, explore the possibilities of his ever growing mental power and meditate about the meaning of 'honor.'

Several days passes with not much to do, so each shift change, Zacxh started a dice game with the objective of loosing a lot of money to engender trust and reduce anxiety among the troops. He knew that he had a mostly unwarranted reputation for brutality and instability. With luck familiarity would reduce that impression. Zacxh really wanted to be able to rely on these elves in a pinch.

Some time later a commotion came from the guard post outside the secret door to the mine entrance which Zacxh and the others moved to investigate. When they arrived at the security outpost, they found Fals the shureken slinger standing opposed by nervous conscripts. Fals appeared to be injured by a few wicked looking whip marks and stood with hands held wide as if to show he was unarmed. The idea of an unarmed member of Loth's fang was of course ludicrous but the stance was at least unthreatening.

Fals explained that he had been expelled from his house and that he had decided to come to the only safe haven he knew. Valron. The story was of course highly suspect to Zacxh, but he held his tongue to see what would come.

To show his good faith, Fals pointed out one of Nal's conscripts and accused him of being a priest. The accusation was as ridiculous as it was terrifying. Zacxh's interest was immediately piqued. He so very hoped to make the acquaintance of an actual priest that he could hardly contain himself. He knew his desire was a heresy that would be punishable by death, but he would love to speak theology to this elf and maybe learn some hidden truths about horrible Loth. If anyone would know them, surely it would be a priest. Loth had no priests of her own, certainly not secret ones traveling as mercenaries so if the accusation was true, Zacxh might have found for himself an interesting conversation.

Needless to say the room erupted. once the dust settled the 'priest' was manacled and interrogated and Fals, who preferred to be called Flick now that her defenses were dropped, was sitting and chatting up Trynn. He was obviously entranced by the beauty and power of his once and future enemy.

In the end of course the 'priest' confessed to heresy and abominations of any sort demanded to avoid being further questioned by Nal and his cruel blade. Zacxh sadly concluded that the poor elf was probably innocent of any priestlyness but he volunteered to make a run to Nerr’tenden anyhow to inform Xulleyl of current events. The run would do him good.

Zacxh set out at an invigorating pace and was well exercised by the time he arrived panting at Xulleyl's door. He knocked and entered when beckoned to find his timing was poor. Xulleyl was in conference with the Steward and Priestess. They all looked at him as if patronizing a child who interrupts the adults at dinner.

The steward made a civil greeting and Xulleyl bade Zach speak.

'Good evening honored Priestess and Steward. I have come to report to my Captain of minor events transpiring in the mines, but clearly I interrupt. You have my deep apology, and I shall return when you have concluded your business.'

The steward's voice was pleasant but insistent. ' Nonsense Zacxh, speak your report. I'm sure we are all interested to hear of your adventures in the mine.'

'Yes. Speak worm.' The priestess was anything but pleasant. Zacxh remembered her cruelty in beating a soup bearer to death, and momentarily envisioned her gouting blood on the end of his sword.

'Certainly honored Priestess, though I fear my report is of little interest. We have captured a spy in the mines. She has made strange accusations of an elf in our company claiming that he is a priest.'

The Loth priestess sprang to her feet and approached in a fury.

'You fool! How could you suppose this was not of the gravest interest to me?!' The priestess was furious and brought her full force of presence to bear. Zacxh dove into his mental fortress and acted cowed to avoid a beating, but continued his pleasing fantasy of her gouting ichors through the mouth.

'Of course oh dread Priestess. How stupid of me. I merely thought it best to gain the captain's opinion on the matter as certainly the accusation is baseless untrue. Such an elf is the stuff of bedtime stories and certainly cannot truly exist.'

The priestess, as always, was livid. but Zacxh hoped she would trip up and confirm the unlikely existence of such a being.

'Stupid male. This is a matter for the church not a pack of ignorant slaves and peons.'

Zacxh saw the priestess anew. She was more than just unstable, she was another piece of damaged goods. Her actions were motivated by more than the needs of order and discipline. She too was insane. Another victim of Loth’s twisted dominion.

Zacxh found himself leading the three lords of Nerr’tenden toward the mines. He cared little for the safety of the priestess, but strode out to point position in order to buffer the Steward and Xulleyl form attack. Part of him believed the whole thing to be a trap designed to lure all the power players of Nerr’tenden out from behind it's gates. He stole a word with his Captain.

'Sir perhaps this a trap designed get all our eggs in one basket...'

Xulleyl looked unconcerned. She cast a rare grin upon Zacxh. 'Not eggs Zacxh. Dragons.'

Zacxh could not suppress a smile and jogged back to his position at the point. As usual, the Captain had a good point.

The scene back at the mining checkpoint was a bit unsettling. Flick and Trynn sat conversing like old lovers. Nal had a wicked look on his face. Valron was visibly upset about something, and the priest had the look of someone about to be thrown into a volcano. His eyes were wide and terrified, his skin pale and clammy, and his manacled hands looked blue and bloodless as they clasped a fresh wound on his thigh. He was clearly going into shock.

The arrival of Zacxh's entourage caused a stir among the conscripts, but the Spiderguard was suspiciously unsurprised; clearly forewarned. Inarra probably had used her psionic arts of foresee the arrival. The 'priest' however could not have been more surprised and terrified if a black dragon had pranced in. His fear was well justified.

Without preamble, the priestess strode up and bashed him fatally to the side of the head with a battered, well used mace. She then bent down to perform some dark prayer on him. She was interrupted, perhaps for the first time in her life. Incredibly, it was Nal.

"Wait!' The look on his face was a mixture of resolve and the realization that he had perhaps just uttered his own death sentence.

The priestess looked at him coolly and finished her prayer. The 'priest's' body healed some and he gasped a rattling breath. The priestess stood and walked like a panther toward Nal. He backpedaled.

'My sincerest apology priestess, I only though that you should hear of my interrogations of this elf before he was put to death. I have administered a thorough investigation upon him, and in my opinion he is no priest...'

The priestess spoke with obvious menace in a voice which brought a cascade of dust from the the unsteady mining rafters above.

'You will never question my actions again! No *male* may presume to interrupt me! Ever!'

She turned from Nal and addressed everyone and no one.

'The fate of this elf is now a matter for the church. I will return with him to Dolthgurdar where he will be beaten and restored and beaten again until the truth is known. Then with no more secrets to reveal, the process will continue until Loth is pleased and satisfied that he has completed his penance. Of course no penance will ever suffice... Yes that is what will happen. it is a good plan. I will leave tomorrow, and I am bringing Zalsh's guard with me. They are in need of a new lieutenant and I will chose one for them. Still she did not seem to be speaking to anyone in particular, Almost as if there were no one in the room worthy of the announcement

The steward stepped in to the conversation, probably sparing Nal's life in the process.

'Wait a minute Honored Priestess. The disposition of this elf is a matter of security, and I am in charge of all internal security here in Nerr’tenden.'

The priestess seemed completely unimpressed.

'Did I not just make my need for a male opinion clear? No steward, I will hang him by the well a few days, then I will take him. He is in the hands of the church now. You will not interfere.'

The steward knew enough to hold his tongue, but Zacxh somehow knew the conversation was far from over. Something outside Zacxh’s purview gave the steward pause in letting her go. For the life of him, Zacxh could not fathom what it could be. Perhaps some problem with a priestess selecting a Lieutenant for Xulleyl. Perhaps that is where Ardul had come from.

The next few hours proceeded predictably. The Spiderguard escorted Flick back to Nerr’tenden and disbanded for a few hours. Nal and Valron consulted in the barracks. Xulleyl prepared for a convoy to Dolthgurdar, and questioned Flick. Zacxh stole away for a much needed bath and a stockade was erected near the well. Things were heating up.

Tension mounted. Valron was clearly upset by the undeserved fate of the non priest and the Steward was upset by the renegade actions of his Priestess. Trynn was exited by the concept of a new training partner and Nal was concerned for his own wellbeing with respect of his actions toward the daughter of Loth. Sorn was unhappy to have to constantly trudge back and forth to and from the dingy mineshaft. and complained about it to anyone who would listen. Only Inarra and Zacxh were unconcerned. Inarra due to her unaffected perhaps flippant nature and Zacxh because he saw the type of tension mounting that might lead to an opportunity for him to purge a cancer form the Underdark. He saw the death of a priestess in the very near future.

All of Nerr’tenden knew that harsh discipline and cruelty at the hands of the church were necessary to maintain order amongst Loth's dark elves. Zacxh knew there had to be a limit; a line between discipline and insanity. Dinlin had clearly stepped over, and Zacxh foresaw a rapidly approaching reckoning. More blood upon the alter of the spider queen.

The thought filled him with hope. The bounds of Spiderguard were loosening and soon he would be free to begin his quest of political and religious reform... A reform that with luck would soon find Dinlin twitching on the end of Zacxh's blade.
