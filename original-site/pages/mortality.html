mortality

Nobody likes to see their character die. However, as you go through your adventures, it is likely that you will face one peril or another; at some point, characters face their final moments.

If your PC is devout and feels that bothering their deity is likely to yield aid instead of divine annoyance, the PC can call out for godly assistance - the chance of success is quite small, but it's there.

For others, or for those whose deity turned away in the critical moment, here's how to think about mortality:

Dark elves are long-lived and skilled with magic. They are also very pragmatic. If your superiors in your house think that you are worth more to them alive than dead than they will consider returning you to life, IF the appropriate spell is handy, IF they feel your death was undeserved and IF they feel like it.

However, if you don't have the ability to repay them for their troubles, you are likely to become indentured for some period of time and/or required to complete some significant task.

To Summarize:

Dying is a bad thing - it's painful on the way out, you get penalized in the game, and then your life is hell when you come back.

Try not to get killed.
