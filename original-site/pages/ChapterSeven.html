ChapterSeven

An Unexpected Return

Zacxh spent the morning among miners. Not many drow would relish the solo trek required outside Nerr’tenden gates to travel to the mines, but Zacxh had made the trip many times and despite the dangers of the Underdark, he enjoyed the solitude of his journey.

Some of the miners remembered Zacxh from earlier encounters. He knew his reputation for destruction and violence preceded him and he traveled armed to the teeth. Despite all that, he treated them as equals and deferred to their expertise in every aspect of planning a mining operation. He had gold to spend, and he wielded it loosely buying high quality food and drink to munch upon during his endless meetings with the miners.

Zacxh's goal was to gain the confidence of these highly skilled workers and promote the most competent of them to foreman for his dig. A few days of social and technical discussions would give him an idea of who was right for the job. He wanted a loyal and proficient crew. Proficiency could be bought with gold, but loyalty would have to be bought with the currency of respect. Zacxh paid it gladly. In fact he found the hardworking and dedicated miners a refreshing change in company from his grumbling and ungrateful companions. Mining is hard honest work, and for that Zacxh loved miners.

The deeply scarred warrior finished a few more casual meetings and decided to head back to the barracks to see what new trouble the Spiderguard was into.

As he approached the gates, it seemed something was wrong. Eight of Lt Ardul's guard were manning the gates and they visibly stiffened as Zacxh approached. He did not know why the guard was doubled or why they should be bridling at his approach. What Zacxh did know was that he would find it exceedingly difficult to bypass the security checkpoint using force. He approached.

'A bit jumpy arent you boys? Are we expecting trouble?' Zacxh walked inside swords reach to show his total lack of concern about his wellbeing. The guards had their hands on weapons and some shifted uneasily.

Their leader spoke:

'Identify yourself!'

Ridiculous, Zacxh thought his face was nothing if not identifiable and this guard, Welcth, was his name, was known to Zacxh.

'You know who I am Weltch, now stand aside, I am on the Captain's business.'

Zacxh Crossed his arms stubbornly. With luck this Guard would get angry and loosen his tongue. Zacxh was still trying to figure out what had Ardul's guards out in force.

The corporal of the guard had different ideas. 'Lay down your weapons and get on your knees'

Zacxh Grinned wickedly and looked from side to side as if to size up his provokers, though in fact he had them well estimated already and was already calculating a set of opening moves. They would make for a tough but not impossible fight.

'You are not going anywhere in this outpost armed and we are going to take you directly to Captain Xulleyl.' The guard seemed steady in his resolve.

Zacxh countered. 'You are Ardul's troop. Bring your lieutenant if you will, but I do NOT take orders from you.'

One of the eight scurried off. Zacxh subtly chanced his attack strategy to reflect the absence.

Ardul arrived only moments later and scowled at the sight of Zacxh.

'I had hoped not to see your face again.' He began.

Zacxh was not in a mood for petty word games. 'Well at least there we are in agreement Lieutenant.'

Ardul was not amused.

'Get on your knees Zacxh and drop your weapons. We will go to Xulleyl together.'

Zacxh thought carefully through his chain of command and decided to take the action most likely to get him to Xulleyl. He laid down his weapons and presented his hands for binding.

Just as he had hoped, Ardul and a pair of guards led Zacxh to Xulleyl. As they walked, Zacxh couldn't help but feel that Ardul was not so sneaky and traitorous as he might at first appear. Sure he was petty and cruel and small, and sure he had harbored traitors (Juell) in his unit. It was also true that his cousin was implicated in the initial mine cave in. But perhaps those things were merely circumstantial. Zacxh would be interested to know Ardul better, though he thought that possibility was remote at best.

Ardul dutifully escorted Zacxh to Xulleyl who, much to Ardul's dismay, dismissed the Lieutenant quickly.

Captain Xulleyl explained to Zacxh that there had been an attempt to poison the well, and it was carried out by an agent impersonating Zacxh.

Zacxh reported his activities in the mine, then was given orders to rejoin the Spiderguard. He bowed out with little ceremony.

He opened the doors with his boot and stormed in enjoying Sorn's glare at his lack of protocol and Nal's displeasure at the sudden lack of oppressive silence. Trynn and Inarra were examining a corpse and Nal was looking coldly at a well beaten looking slave.

Zacxh boomed out. 'So, did I die well?'

The question went unanswered and Zacxh went mostly ignored. Apparently, the Spiderguard once again was blind to his dark humor. Perhaps as was so often the case only he could understand the irony. Zacxh exhaled, disappointed but hardly surprised, and pressed on. He wondered if his humor was really as droll as they all seemed to imply.

The Spiderguard barracks was in typical disarray. No one had thought to clear the dining table, and consequently it was festooned with half consumed loaves of bread and moldering cheeses. Bottles and corks and the varying detritus of a table whose contents nourish the body but not the spirit lay strewn about. The sorry meal had been picked at like a corpse is picked apart by rodents, that is without regard to anything but the physical need to feed.

Zacxh curled his lip then turned to the task of cleaning it all up. As he did, he took in the intrigues and discussions of the evening. They revolved around how to best respond to the foiled plot to poison the well.

Zacxh looked at the poisoned slave, then at the table. He regarded the rapidly stiffening corpse in the center of the room then regarded the slowly warming cheese. He looked at the feces near the dead elf woman with the shimmering skin then regarded his dirty hands and nails. He was repulsed.

Zacxh decided there and then to stop imbibing of this world like some savage beast and backed from the table to wash up in a nearby bowl. He then removed all his gear and scrubbed it furiously. He cleaned and sharpened and oiled as if tending to the implements of royalty, and in his mind, he was, for he tended to himself.

When all was said and done, Zacxh regarded his masterwork weapons. They had been specially made and balanced for him. He regarded his well kept armor, his blades, his bow. Then he regarded his scrubbed arms and hands. Weapons all. Nothing more, nothing less. He would only expose them as necessary to vanquish his foes and nourish his spirit. He would keep them pure. he would eat only the most uplifting meals and distain all else. He would master his bodies urges not let them master him.

At the edge of the table stood the last unopened bottle of good wine. he searched for a vessel and at last settled upon a tiny glass goblet.

He gathered his 'meal' and ritualistically cleaned the glass until it shone.

He lifted his draught in salute to himself and his rebirth as something pure and ritualistically tossed back the mediocre drink. It was good but not great. A bit fouled by the cork. It marked his last act of animal indulgence.

Zacxh regarded his comrades. they saw no change in him, but deep down something inside him changed. He closed his eyes and focused upon it.

Thirst was gone. Hunger, gone. Lust, gone. His animal connection to and hunger for this putrid world, gone.

Zacxh took one last look at the sad table and grinned. Then turned his attention back to the discussions of the evening.

At length, it was jointly decided to make the impression that the well was spoiled.

To that end, Captain Xulleyl suggested that he Spiderguard lead a caravan of supposed water gatherers out of Nerr’tenden. meanwhile Xulleyl would set up an ambush within the city. With luck, the caravan would convince Orllylyl or whoever the well prisoners were that Nerr’tenden was poisoned and that it was time to attack (and fall into the ambush within Nerr’tenden)

Valron and Nal devised and approved the plan with little input from the unit , a fact which suited Zacxh just fine. He was tired of political posturing and looked forward to his next mission. He needed practice with his blades and had a few new moves to try in actual combat. Valron and Trynn had recently lost their interest in sparring with him. Perhaps he would have to find some training partners outside the Guard...

Zacxh retired to his quarters feeling both empty of food and drink, and at the same time more pure somehow, He could hardly wait to void his bowls in the morning, Then he could begin to fully divest himself of the animal side of his being. He wondered how his new form of mental sustenance would affect his physical form. Would he dry up and become but a walking sinew? Would his strength leave him. Perhaps he would just fall over and die.

Soon he would know.

Morning came quickly for Zacxh. He had sat and crossed his legs and dropped into a deep trance. He relived in memory every action of the day. When he came to, he smiled, pleased with his actions. He felt as though he were on the road to something great. Almost as if destiny were calling. He cleaned himself meticulously, and put a keen edge along with a drop of oil on his blades. He shaved his face and head clean then adjoined to the common room. he was one of the first. The others joined soon enough and soon some ogres a few conscripts were unknowingly poisoned by Nal and the Spiderguard moved out the gates. Zacxh and Trynn took the lead. the scouted ahead along with Jane, Inarra's soul stone.

Half a days march outside Nerr’tenden, They encountered Fals or at least someone much like him. He had ??? pinned down and close to dealt with those damn shurikin. This time the Spiderguard, at least Trynn and .Zacxh attacked. Trynn got there first and Zacxh immediately behind. Fals knocked Trynn silly in an instant, and just as Zacxh was preparing to strike, Valron issued a command to stop fighting. Everyone, Fals included, obeyed and a truce was negotiated.

At length it was negotiated that the caravan would return to Nerr’tenden. Valron sealed the deal. and the Spiderguard returned victorious. the rues had worked and fals' presence in the corridors was good intel about who was behind the attacks on the well. On the return, the poisoned conscripts grew visibly ill. Zacxh was sure that fact did not go unnoticed by the attacking force. The attack would come soon, and Nerr’tenden was ready.

Zacxh found a concealed spot in the main courtyard to sit and wait. He expected the assault to open with massive flame and destruction and did not want to miss the show.

It had been more than a day since his last sip of sustenance, and he felt strong and vital. Pure.

Zacxh longed for the fight to come.
