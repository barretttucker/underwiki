CHAPTER 0. THE GREAT ESCAPE

“Crack!... Crack! Crack!” Lucinda’s vicious snake headed whip bit again and again. “Crack” “You ungrateful little bastard! Don’t you even have the sense to cry out. You would deprive your mistress of the pleasure of your song!!! Well, I will see to it that you sing.” “Crack!” Zacxh knew his reaction or, in this case, a lack thereof would have little effect on the outcome of this beating. He had been whipped too many times to be conned by the enraged words of a priestess. She would whip him until either he fell insensible or until she found something else to appease her cruelty. In Lucinda’s case it would probably be the former. She loved to see a male fall victim to her wrath. “Crack, Crack, Crack…” “Scream you insolent bastard!!!” A few years ago he might have even done it. He might have thought that by screaming he would appease her in some way. It could work on mistress Serinill, maybe on Mistress Theria too. But this Lucinda, well she was a real piece of work. His only defense would be his inevitable weakness. Therein lay the problem. His weakness was beginning to fail him. His body had grown large and tough it is true, but that was not it. Larger more formidable males would have collapsed by now. Zacxh had found a ‘hiding spot’ in his mind. It was strange, and in a way he knew it had been there all along, but now he could run there and take shelter at will. He used to feel a great sense of despair and loss when his body was beaten and deformed. It was just that attachment that had concealed the hiding spot from him. He now knew that his body was not related to his mind, but was simply its vehicle. Today His vehicle was going to be beaten into a bloody rag. “Crack” “Fall you insolent pig” Zacxh could hear the Snake headed whip. He could, in a detached way, feel the painful neurotoxin of the vipers' every bite. But the pain lived in his nerves and his brain. It no longer penetrated his mind. Unfortunately, that meant that his battered, branded, tattooed, and pierced frame would have to fend for itself. Loss of blood and shock would eventually bring it down, though it would take a while. This was not pleasing to the Mistress. Actually she had found other more satisfying subjects for her pleasure, and seemed almost apathetic and mechanical in her abuse. “Crack!” “I will flay your flesh and feed it to the worgs you insolent pig, now cry out your devotion to me or DIE!!!” Zacxh smiled instead. He found it sad and slightly amusing that she was so disinterested in his torture that she had called him ‘pig” again. She had definitely lost interest. It hadn’t always been that way. For a time, he had even been favored by the priestess of House Oussund. They had utterly destroyed his family in a raid to raise the status of their house. He was so young then. Barely even a boy. He had been born into the role of Guard-leader aspirant, and had his house survived, he would have had an entire section under his command. He would have been killing priestesses in routine raids and watching *them* cry out for mercy. Ah well. His lot was not so bad. In fact, he had recently come to appreciate his position. He was a pit fighter; an anonymous champion of House Oussund. As such, great care was taken to ensure he was as prepared as possible for his next bout. As a child he had learned to pounce on other boys... and orcs and lizard folk quickly and quietly. He knew just where to slide his knife to still a beating heart or disable a troublesome sword arm. He proved an able and recorseful fighter and had killed off all his kin in the first year or so of his enslavement. He was strong, cunning and quick in combat and thus proved to be of great value to House Oussund. Like any prize, they kept him in good repair, or at least fully functional. His missing digits were painfully and grudgingly replaced by chiding priestesses who beat him senseless with clubs and whips for the favor. His right ear was but a tattered rag and his ebony skin was so stitched with grey scars that it was difficult, in some areas, to find any virgin flesh at all. His knuckles were thick and knobby from repeated breaking and his nose was flat and round like an orc’s. His tattoos and marks of ownership were all but indiscernible anymore, lost in the tattered menagerie of his flesh Yes, She would beat him into a pulpy, bloody wreck. Then he would be salved and stitched up by some servant and left to his fevers and dementias. He would lie in a painful ball for days or weeks and would eventually come to his senses. Then a bout. Would it be a beast again? Maybe a young troll? He would be given a spear or sword, sometimes a net or sharpened stick. He would be cast into darkness or blinding, painful light and shoved into some cramped space to fight to the death. Then, as a reward for victory, he would be forced to couple with some priestess or another who would chastise and goad him for his lack of enthusiasm then proceed to beat him again into a bleeding, retching, discarded heap of flesh. It was an endless cycle and Zacxh knew it would eventually end in death. Zacxh had grown accustomed to the savage cycle of victory, punishment and reward and had actually begun to look forward to his fights, but now he knew he was in danger. His lack of care for his body’s wellbeing was beginning to show, and so he no longer was of use to the priestesses of Loth. He would soon be sold to the mines or, perhaps, tortured to death or sacrificed in some way. He knew his end was near. Zacxh surmised that the most likely form of his demise would be some skilled or overpowering adversary in the pit. His priestesses would drug or disable him in some way and bet heavily against his victory. He had been so sucessful in the pit, that they could easily make a heafty profit from his defeat. Perhaps, he thought, his time was at hand. He had heard whisperings that he was to be wrapped in leather armor, masked to disguise his wounds, and sent into the pit against Gruulosh, the renowned and reportedly unbeatable champion of another house. If true, He was doomed. His arms and legs were hanging limp and near lifeless. Even the dangerous venom of a green mobeus spider would barely stimulate him enough to walk. He could perhaps muster enough strength to feebly clutch his spear and drag himself about, but there would be no power in him. Yes, Zacxh thought, at long last, this was probably going to be his fated day. So lost in his conjecture was Zacxh, that he barely noticed, when the beating stopped. Where had Lucinda gone? He pulled himself up and found that the door to the arena was not open, and there were no weapons or armor about. Perhaps he would not fight today. Instead, the passage to the observing deck lay open. Was he meant to climb the stair and watch a bout? He struggled with the idea a moment. He had never watched one before, but soon crept up to a private observation booth. No one would see him here and there was a note addressed to him on the seat...

Two combatants entered the small private arena. All around were other discreet observation booths shrouded in darkness. How many hidden Elves were watching? One of the combatants was a large and only slightly drugged ogre. The other had the same build and stance as Zacxh. Both warriors were armed with small sharp pikes and masked to hide their faces. The elf was scarred and torn of body and watching him was like looking in a reflecting pool. The combat was short. The Dark elf died on the ogre’s pike after only a single failed parry. Zacxh sighed and opened the note...

Zacxh wasted no time leaving the arena. He knew the twisting hallways which wound out of house Ossund, but since his childhood eighty some years hence he had never followed them all the way through to the door.. He reread his note. Without giving a reason, It instructed him to leave in secrecy and meet a caravan, but getting to the crystal gate would be no easy task. He was a secret slave of house Ossund, not meant to be wandering the halls, and worse yet, he was presumed dead. Perhaps, he thought, he should try to escape via the service exit near the kitchen. He noted wryly that he was unarmed and wearing only his soft boots and a bloodstained loincloth. Zacxh clung to seldom used corridors with his torn ears tuned to every possible source of danger. Several times he had to dive into a side chamber or hide in a tight corner to avoid discovery. By the time he reached the kitchens he was shaking with tension. A few servants were working to prepare the middle meal for some house guards. They did not notice as Zacxh stole quietly into their presence. He crammed his body into a tight space between a scalding hot oven and a prep table. The oven toasted his flesh slightly as he waited and watched the comings and goings of the kitchen staff. He could not be discovered, so he waited until there was a break between servings. When only one Servant remained and was immersed in the task of washing pots, Zacxh made his escape. He grabbed a sturdy looking kitchen knife, and slipped out the delivery door. Zacxh stopped outside the kitchen long enough to dig through a trash bin. He dug out an old filthy cloak and a tired leather belt into which he tucked his fine carving knife. The cloak had a hood and, though ill fitting of his pit fighter's war shaped frame, it concealed his scarred and tattooed skin. He had little trouble, thus adorned, blending into the traffic which would conceil his passage through the crystal gate. True to the prediction of the mysterious scroll, there was a caravan in need of laborers making for Nerr'tenden. A stern and impatient looking caravan leader tapped her foot and rolled her eyes despairingly at Zacxh's approach. As the caravan made preparations to leave, two other outsiders joined the group. The one who tersly introduced himself as Nal, was a handsome but tight lipped elf adorned in Fine black traveling cloths and a wearing creepy black short blade. The pommel was decorated with a set of clenched teeth which seemed to grip the blade. The overall effect of his good looks, cruel eyes, and macobre choice of armament was definately disturbing. The other, Valron, was a strong, well groomed Fighter wearing the uniform of a house guard. He carried a large wicked looking trident, and his body language was loose and comfortable. He carried his weapon with a careless ease which bespoke a deadly familiarity. In Valron, Zacxh's experienced eye beheld a formidable warrior. The caravan guards ignored the newcomers for the most part, though as they made final preparations, Rath, the caravan's leader, addressed them curtly. She concisely told them that they were expected to pay for their own rations and carry their own weight. She informed them that she was only allowing for their presence on the off chance they might prove useful as fodder in any unforeseen skirmishes which may lay ahead. Zacxh merely nodded his head at the instructions and tried to keep out of the way as the caravan finally moved out. The ominous well dressed traveler kept to himself for the most part. The well groomed fighter spoke a bit with the guards and toyed with a bandoleer of small vials hanging across his chest. Zacxh stayed near a line of chained dueagar slaves who shuffled along bearing gear and cargo. For the first few days the caravan moved along the Underdark with good speed and in relative safety. Rath made several stops at increasingly small outposts picking up supplies and making deliveries. One town (?), several days into the trek, was famous for tea. Rath allowed a night for the travelers to enjoy a relaxing and highly traditional tea ceremony and a good meal. Zacxh traded his only valuable possession, the fine kitchen knife to a sour faced cook for a poor pot of thin stew and a rusted and dull dagger with a greasy and worn scabbard. Still the weapon had a nice balance and after countless hours of rubbing and scraping, it cleaned up well enough to be serviceable. Rath provided sparse rations to her crew as they continued generally north. Both of the other newcomers, the creepy eyed dandy and the wellspoken guard, seemed a bit put off by the cheap rations, but to Zacxh each meal was a feast. Whatever the scriber of the note had in store, these days were a happy time. Freedom from the house walls, plentiful food and tea... It was a long overdue turn of fate. Zacxh looked down at his heavily scarred arms and hands. They were an ugly reminder of the careless cruelty that even now Zacxh was beginning to purge from his mind. All that pain, anger and viciousness was leaving his conscious mind and taking up residence... somewhere else. He wondered if someday it might rear up and manifest itself in some physical way. Certainly he had scars. Noble Valron made several friendly remarks to Zacxh and seemed generally personable but Nal, the dark brooding elf kept mostly to himself. For his part, Zacxh lied that he was a servant performing a menial errand for his house and tried to avoid saying anything which might betray the fact that he was in fact, an escaped slave and fugitive. After days of rapid travel, the group entered some seldom used backwater caverns which were reportedly a 'shortcut' toward Nerr’tenden. Eventually, after a number of turns and twists that left Zacxh completely disoriented, they happened upon a slaughtered caravan. The bodies of a dozen or so guards were strewn into a shallow uncovered grave. As (?) the scout pushed ahead to look for an ambush, Zacxh pillaged the dead for scraps of workable armor and weapons. He quickly strapped on a mismatched suit consisting of Leather greaves and gauntlets, a boiled leather jerkin and bloodied bracers. He found two decent long thin blades and strapped them, crossing, to his back. He also found a nice hand crossbow and set a short quiver of bolts on his left hip. He replaced the scabbard but kept his own dagger. It had finally come to a fine edge and was Zacxh's first real possession in the world. As an afterthought, Zacxh collected up a few coins and a small pouch to put them in, ignoring the disgusted faces of his traveling companions. They were the first coins he had owned in eighty some years. Zacxh emerged from the shallow pit armed, wealthy, and happy. For the first time in ages he felt free. He was elated, and most surprising, was eager for the fight ahead. He was armed, by choice this time, and if he were to fight, he enjoyed the prospect of a true victory, not one sullied by drugging, poor equipment, or other underhanded tampering. Just as Zacxh was tightening the last straps of his armor, (scout) the scout returned with news of an ambush ahead. He led the guards down a side hallway and as Zacxh, Valron and Nal made to follow, Rath quickly deputized them and admonished them to 'Stay out of the way and not do anything stupid.' Zacxh had seldom heard less needed advice. (scout) led the way, pushing about fifty feet ahead. He poked his head around a turn in the naturally formed stone and, seeing no threat, stepped around. The sound of four crossbow strings heralded the arrival of well aimed bolts which jerked and slammed (scout's) body. A gout of greenish foam erupted from his mouth as he fell lifeless to the floor. The guards seemed shocked and confused a moment, so Zacxh acted first. He remembered an old elf trick a fellow slave had taught him and brought a deepened darkness about the fallen scout. Then, taking advantage of the gloomy cover, Zacxh pulled (scout) back around the corner and out of the line of fire. More bolts were fired blindly into the darkness but they merely clacked ineffectually on the stone around them. Zacxh took a quick look at (scout) and realized he was beyond help. The green ooze dribbling from the jovial elf's lips spelled doom. Zacxh emptied his thoughts and let himself slip into the 'hidden place' in his mind. He became a being beyond pain. His senses became detached and super acute, and he became so attuned to the movement and forces around himself that he felt he could 'see' right into the most vital spots of those around him. The places on their bodies where their energies converged. Zacxh charged... And, as he broke free of the globe of darkness, found himself joined by Guards Valron and Nal. Only the slaves and a pair of attending guards had stayed behind. The melee lasted only a moment. Zacxh and Valron quickly dispatched a pair of ambushers and the guards fightin allongside nal Nal felled the other two, yet this was merely a delaying force. The chamber narrowed and led around another bend. Zacxh and the guards joined Rath and entered a large circular chamber. It seemed empty and anticlimactic. One of the guards motioned to the south wall indicating the presence of some suspicious looking cracks. Zacxh, two guards and Rath went over to have a look and were suddenly caught in the thick sticky strands of a spell web. Crossbow bolts flew 'through' the northern wall, and with them came the realization that the wall was illusory, merely a trick of magic, perspective and the dim shadows of photo luminescent fungi. Three figures were concealed by that wall. Two fired hand crossbows into the captive guards and one was preparing another incantation like the one which had created the webs. Zacxh struggled in vain to force his way free of his entanglement while watching Valron ram her trident lethally onto one of the crossbowman. The mage finished his incantation and vanished from view at about the same moment Zacxh finally tore free of the webs and partially dodged a hastily aimed bolt. The tiny, likely poisoned missile managed to nick his side as it deflected off his boiled leather breastplate. Zacxh never noticed his slight wound, and drove the point of his rapier into the belly of the remaining crossbowman. The doomed elf scooped at his spilling guts and crumpled to the ground as all the color drained from his face. Nal called out "Valron come to me and we will block the escape. Let the others flush him out. All sound stopped and the party collectively held their breath listening for some sign of the illusive mage. "Infernus arachnidious!" The mage popped back into view as a blindingly bright fan of flames leapt from his splayed fingers and into the guardsmen still trapped in the thick sticky strands of the web. "FOOF!" The webs caught aflame and instantly went up in a smoky greasy explosion of heat. Rath dove free of the worst of the flames, but both the male guards shreaked horribly as flesh dripped molten off of their faces and limbs. They died screaming and curling into blackened, gory horrors. Rath, Zacxh and Nal descended upon the murdering mage with the fury of a cave in. Blows came from all sides and it was only a moment before the mage was trussed and tied to a pole. Nal seemed to relish his cruel interrogation of the prisoner and Zach left him to the grisly work in order to search the cave. Zacxh poked around the cave looking for whatever could have put these brigands into such a defensive posture. He found two large sacks in the corner and was struck momentarily speechless as he discovered they contained a huge fortune in Semi cut rubies. Many were nearly priceless, as large as a fist. "Hey Rath, we have a situation here... " Zacxh wanted to be wealthy just as well as the next, but all but two of the guards were dead. The chest was large, and they were still a good couple days of travel from Nerr’tenden. This cache of wealth meant pursuit and suspicion and untold danger. Even worse, Rath explained that the rubies were identifiably cut and marked with the symbol of the House Godendar ruby mines at Tleth'tyrr. Trouble. Whoever turned up with this treasure would likely not live long enough to spend it The diminished party of six warriors and the six slaves set out soon thereafter. They shortened the shackles of the Dueagar and loaded them with a gagged and bound mage as well as an unbelievable treasure in rubies. Zacxh persuaded Rath to lend him (scouts) enchanted cloak of concealment and used it to scout ahead of the vulnerable convoy for trouble. The caravan move quickly and only stopped often enough to keep the slaves from dying from exhaustion in their boots. As Rath feared, the deep dwarves could not resist the temptation of all those rubies. All but two, as well as one of Rath”s trusted guards died when the hopeless bastards tried to make their escape. The remaining two were assigned to carry the captive mage. The caravan which rolled to the gates of Nerr’tenden was but a shadow of the one which departed the Crystal Gate. A guard and an officer greeted the ragged band as they approached the gate. The officer wore a 'spider pin'. Zacxh was relieved to be done with the poorly run caravan and never looked back as he walked straight up to the lieutenant and presented him with the stone token he received with his note. It was accepted silently. Valron and Nal walked up and each produced similar stones. They were taken. The lieutenant seemed amused by the haggard looking caravan and after only a moment led the way into a heavily fortified keep. He tersly introduced himself as 'Lieutenant Ardul' and seemed ungrateful for the rubies which, as a token of good faith and to be rid of their inherent danger, Rath at the advice of Zacxh, Valron and Nal gifted to house Oussund. He said they would bring nothing but trouble as they were ransomed back to their rightful owners. He sequestered the guard and Rath in a separate room, and made it fairly clear that they would not live to see another day. Zacxh Valron and Nal were brought to a small barracks chamber and locked in. Apparently they had found their new home. Nal bunked alone in one of the double rooms, and Valron and Zacxh took a room together. It looked like there was room for eight in the squad bay, and it seemed to Zacxh that he could trust the former Oussund house guard to watch his back. At least they had shared the road together, and they shared a confidence in the secret of the rubies. He seemed a quiet but steady enough roommate. The Lieutenant came in awhile later. He apprised the trio of travelers warily before speaking. "Well it seems you were able to arrive in one piece which is more than can be said for the balance of your caravan... You should choose quarters and settle in. The rest will arrive soon..." With that, he tossed each of the three a large ruby in the rough. They were cut only enough to transport to skilled shapers but each bore the inscription of hoise godendor. "Let these be a reminder to you of your trials along the road and of the danger you faced in taking that treasure. You were right to give them up as they are a political hot potato and would surely have burnt you had you opted to fence them yourself. The less said of them, the better.'

The lieutenant left without further ado.

Another Scarred Elf arrived a few hours later. He was blindfolded and exhibited the abused marks of a slave. He introduced himself as Trynn but said little else.
