ChapterOne

Zacxh wasted no time leaving the arena. He knew the twisting hallways which wound out of house Ossund, but since his childhood eighty some years hence he had never followed them all the way through the door.. Zacxh reread his note. He was instructed to leave and meet a caravan but getting to the crystal gate would be no easy task. He was a secret slave of house Ossund not meant to be wandering the halls, and worse yet, he was presumed dead. Perhaps, he thought, he should try to escape via the service exit near the kitchen. He was unarmed and wearing only his soft boots and a bloodstained loincloth.

Zacxh clung to seldom used corridors with his torn ears tuned to every possible source of danger. Several times he had to dive into a side chamber or hide in a tight corner to avoid discovery. By the time he reached the kitchens he was nearly shaking with tension.

A few servants were working to prepare the middle meal for some house guards. They did not notice as Zacxh stole quietly into their presence. Zacxh crammed his body into a tight space between a scalding hot oven and a prep table. The oven toasted his flesh slightly as he waited and watched the comings and goings of the kitchen staff. He could not be discovered, so waited until there was a break between servings.

When only one Servant remained and was immersed in the task of washing pots, Zacxh made his escape. He grabbed a sturdy looking kitchen knife, and slipped out the delivery door.

Zacxh stopped outside the kitchen long enough to dig through a trash bin. He dug out an old filthy cloak and a tired leather belt into which he tucked his fine carving knife. The cloak had a hood and though ill fitting of his pit fighter's war shaped frame, it concealed his scarred and tattooed skin. He had little trouble, thus adorned, blending into the traffic leading out the crystal gate.

True to the prediction of the mysterious scroll, there was a caravan in need of laborers making for Nerr'tenden. A stern and impatient looking caravan leader tapped her foot and rolled her eyes despairingly at Zacxh's approach.

As the caravan made preparations to leave, other wanderers also joined the group. One was a Striking elf wearing Fine black traveling cloths and a creepy black blade. The pommel was decorated with a set of clenched teeth which seemed to grip the blade. Another was a strong well groomed Fighter wearing the uniform of a house guard. He carried a large wicked looking trident. The fighter looked loose and comfortable and carried his weapon with a careless ease which bespoke a deadly familiarity with the weapon.

The caravan guards ignored the newcomers for the most part. As they made final preparations, Rath, the caravan's leader, addressed them curtly. She concisely told them that they were expected to pay for their own rations and carry their own weight, and that she was only allowing for their presence on the off chance they might prove useful as fodder in any unforeseen skirmishes which lay ahead.

Zacxh merely nodded his head at the instructions and tried to keep out of the way as the caravan finally moved out. The handsome well dressed traveler kept to himself for the most part and the fighter spoke a bit with the guards and toyed a bit with a bandoleer of small vials hanging across his chest. Zacxh stayed near a line of chained dueagar slaves who shuffled along bearing gear and cargo.

For the first few days the caravan moved along the Underdark with good speed and in relative safety. Rath made several stops at increasingly small outposts picking up supplies and making deliveries.

One town (?) was famous for tea and Rath allowed a night for the travelers to enjoy the ceremonies and have a good meal. Zacxh traded his only valuable possession, the fine kitchen knife to a sour faced cook for a poor pot of thin stew and a rusted and dull dagger with a greasy and worn scabbard. Still the weapon had a nice balance and after countless hours of rubbing and scraping cleaned up well enough to be serviceable.

Rath provided sparse rations to her crew as they continued generally north. Both of the other newcomers, the creepy eyed dandy and the guard seemed a bit put off by the cheap rations, but to Zacxh each meal was a feast. (Anita) made several friendly remarks to Zacxh and seemed generally friendly but the dark brooding elf kept mostly to himself. For his part, Zacxh lied that he was a servant performing a menial errand for his house and tried to avoid saying anything which might betray the fact that he was an escaped slave and a fugitive.

Eventually, the group happened upon a slaughtered caravan. Bodies of a dozen or so guards were strewn into a shallow uncovered grave. As (?) the scout pushed ahead to look for an ambush, Zacxh pillaged the dead for scraps of workable armor and weapons. He quickly strapped on a mismatched suit consisting of Leather greaves and gauntlets, a boiled leather jerkin and bloodied bracers. He found two decent long thin blades and strapped them, crossing, to his back. He also found a nice hand crossbow and set a short quiver of bolts on his left hip. He replaced the scabbard but kept his own dagger as it had finally come to a fine edge and was Zacxh's first real possession in the world. As an afterthought, Zacxh collected up a few coins and a small pouch to put them in ignoring the disgusted faces of his traveling companions. They were the first coins he had owned in eighty some years.

Zacxh emerged from the shallow pit armed, wealthy, and happy. For the first time in ages he felt free. He was elated, and most surprising, was eager for the fight ahead. He was armed, by choice this time, and if he were to fight, he enjoyed the prospect of a true victory, not one sullied by drugging, poor equipment, or other underhanded tampering.

Just as Zacxh was tightening the last straps of his armor, (scout) the scout returned with news of an ambush ahead. He led the guards down a side hallway and as Zacxh Valron and Nal made to follow, Rath quickly deputized them and admonished them to 'Stay out of the way and not do anything stupid.' Zacxh had seldom heard less needed advice.

(scout) led the way, pushing about fifty feet ahead. He poked his head around a turn in the naturally formed stone and seeing no threat stepped around. The sound of four crossbow strings heralded the arrival of well aimed bolts which jerked and slammed (scout's) body. A gout of greenish foam erupted from his mouth as he fell lifeless to the floor.

The guards seemed shocked and confused a moment, so Zacxh acted first. He remembered an old elf trick a fellow slave had taught him and brought a deepened darkness about the fallen scout then, taking advantage of the gloomy cover, Zacxh pulled (scout) back around the corner and out of the line of fire. More bolts fired blindly into the darkness clacked ineffectually on the stone around them.

Zacxh took a quick look at (scout) and realized he was beyond help. Green ooze dribbling from the jovial elf's lips spelled doom.

Zacxh emptied his mind and let himself slip into that 'hidden place' in his mind. He became a being beyond pain. His senses became detached and super acute, and he would be so attuned as to be able to drive his blade right into the most vital spots of his foes.

Zacxh charged... And, as he broke free of his globe of darkness, found himself surrounded by guards and the other caravan joiners. Only the slaves and a pair of attending guards had stayed behind.

The melee lasted only a moment. Zacxh and Valron quickly dispatched a pair of ambushers and the guards and (andrew) felled the other two, yet this was merely a delaying force. The chamber narrowed and led around another bend. Zacxh and the guards joined Rath and entered a large circular chamber. It seemed empty and anticlimactic.

One of the guards motioned to the south wall indicating the presence of some suspicious looking cracks. Zacxh, two guards and Rath went over to have a look and were suddenly caught in the thick sticky strands of a web. Crossbow bolts flew 'through' the northern wall, and with them came the realization that the wall was illusory, merely a trick of magic, perspective and the dim shadows of photo luminescent fungi.

Three figures were concealed by that wall. Two fired hand crossbows into the captive guards and one was preparing another incantation like the one which had created the webs.

Zacxh struggled in0vain to force his way free of his entanglement while watching Valron ram her trident lethally onto one of the crossbowman.

The mage finished his incantation and vanished from view at about the same moment Zacxh finally tore free of the webs and partially dodged a hastily aimed bolt. The tiny, likely poisoned missile managed to nick his side as it deflected off his boiled leather breastplate. Zacxh never noticed his slight wound, and drove the point of his rapier into the belly of the remaining crossbowman. The doomed elf scooped at his spilling guts and crumpled to the ground as all the color drained from his shocked face.

Nal called out "Valron come to me and we will block the escape. Let the others flush him out. All sound stopped and the party collectively held their breath listening for some sign of the illusive mage.

"Infernus arachnidious!" The mage popped back into view as a blindingly bright fan of flames leapt from his splayed fingers and into the guardsmen still trapped in the thick sticky strands of the web.

"FOOF!" The webs caught aflame and instantly went up in a smoky greasy explosion of heat. Rath dove free of the worst of the flames, but both the male guards streaked horribly as flesh dripped molten off of their faces and limbs. They died screaming and curling into blackened gory horrors.

Rath, Zacxh and Nal descended upon the murdering mage with the fury of a cave in. Blows came from all sides and it was only a moment before the mage was trussed and tied to a pole. Nal seemed to relish his cruel interrogation of the prisoner and Zach left him to the grisly work in order to search the cave.

Zacxh poked around the cave looking for whatever could have put these brigands into such a defensive posture. He found two large sacks in the corner and was struck momentarily speechless as he discovered they contained a huge fortune in Semi cut rubies. Some as large as a fist.

"Hey Rath, we have a situation here... " Zacxh wanted to be wealthy just as well as the next, but all but two of the guards were dead. The chest was large, and they were still a good days of travel from (destination). This cache of wealth meant pursuit and suspicion and untold danger. Even worse, Rath explained that the rubies were identifiably cut and marked with the symbol of the House Godendar ruby mines at Tleth'tyrr. These were trouble.

The diminished party of six warriors and the six slaves set out soon thereafter. They shortened the shackles of the Dueagar and loaded them with a gagged and bound mage as well as an unbelievable treasure in rubies. Zacxh persuaded Rath to lend him (scouts) enchanted cloak of concealment and used it to scout ahead of the vulnerable convoy for trouble.

As Rath feared, the deep dwarves could not resist the temptation of all those rubies. All but two, as well as one of Rath”s trusted guards died the following morning when the hopeless bastards tried to make their escape. The remaining two were assigned to carry the captive mage, discarded in the combat. The caravan which rolled to the gates of (new barracks) was merely a shadow of the one which departed (crystal gate city)

A guard and an officer greeted the ragged band as they approached the gate. The officer wore a 'spider pin'. Zacxh was relieved to be done with the poorly run caravan and never looked back as he walked straight up to the lieutenant and presented him with the stone token he received with his note. It was accepted silently. Valron and Nal walked up and each produced similar stones. They were taken.

The lieutenant seemed amused by the haggard looking caravan and after only a moment led the way into a heavily fortified keep.

The Lieutenant seemed ungrateful for the rubies which as a token of good faith, and to be rid of their inherent danger Rath at the advice of Zacxh Valron and Nal gifted to house Oussund. He said they would bring nothing but trouble as they were ransomed back to their rightful owners.

He sequestered the guard and Rath in a separate room, and made it fairly clear that they would not live to see another day. Zacxh Valron and Nal were brought to a small barracks chamber and locked in. Apparently they had found their new home. Nal bunked alone in one of the double bunk rooms, and Valron and Zacxh took a room together. It looked like there was room for eight in the squad bay, and it seemed to Zacxh that he could trust the former Oussund house guard to watch his back. At least they had shared the road together, and they shared a confidence in the secret of the rubies. He seemed a quiet but steady enough roommate.

The Lieutenant came in awhile later. He apprised the trio of travelers warily before speaking.

"Well it seems you were able to arrive in one piece which is more than can be said for the balance of your caravan... You should choose quarters and settle in the rest will arrive soon..."

With that, he tossed each of the three a large ruby in the rough.

"Let these be a reminder to you of your trials along the road and of the danger you faced in taking that treasure. You were right to give them up as they are a political hot potato and would surely have burnt you had you opted to fence them yourself.

The lieutenant left without further ado.
