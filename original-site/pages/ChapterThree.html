CHAPTER 3. REUNION

Xulleyl poured over the maps looking for the possible location of the missing mage and another headquarters. A likely spot was found on one of the maps and the trio departed with haste.

They backtracked to a secret door near the location of their first melee. The well hidden door was found, but search as they may, no way to open it presented itself. Frustrated, they hid themselves as best they may and resolved to wait the door out.

An hour or so passed before their patience was rewarded. Sorn poked his head carefully out of the troublesome door. Captain Xulleyl took a step to announce herself, but without a look, Sorn hurled a spell. Stringy magical energy danced harmlessly around the Captain apparently causing no damage.

Xulleyl pushed past Sorn who dropped horrified and prostrate to the ground.

'Nice trick' Her voice dripped with irony.

The balance of the Spidergaurd were all in this larger storeroom. They looked battle worn and were in various states of repose poking through stores and what was likely loot from fallen enemies. Kadyel made a report of their battles and was sure to point out that only through her leadership and battle prowess was the group persuaded to come this far. She clearly stated that the group was disinclined to press into the mines after their first encounter. Indeed Trynn looked uncomfortable in these surroundings.

Zacxh was saddened that the warrior was so skittish. He wondered if perhaps Trynn's spirit had been broken by his beatings in house Ossund just as Zacxh's mind and body had been steeled.

The Captain took charge of her uncertain rabble of a unit. Zacxh stayed in the main chamber with Trynn, Inarra and Valron. Nal, Kadyel and the captain took a well beaten captive to a back room to be 'questioned' by Inarra and Nal. Apparently she had a penchant for interrogation of a gentle sort and Nal's technique was more messy.

Zacxh spoke with those not involved with the questioning and together they pieced together what both parties had heard and encountered of forces in the mines. Apparently there was at least one more contingent wandering around under the command of a sergeant and a Captain 'Leal'

Zacxh was preparing to scout out this missing group when the interrogators emerged with little new to report. The only new information was that a delivery of poison was expected and that the Melted hallway was caused by a mage which seemed to be attacking the mine invaders. No one knew what the mage looked like or where he might be now.

The party resolved to camp down for a while and then go searching after some rest. Zacxh was injured from his fights, but tended to the wounds of his charges rather than his own. He knew his body could probably afford to go without rest for a little longer. Some of the others in his party seemed much the worse for wear.

Some hours passed when a rhythmic knock came on the secret door.

Elves scrambled about the storeroom, and Zacxh was about to charge out and surprise attack the knocker when Kadyel stopped him with a gesture. The voice which came from her was masculine and strange.

She used some names and bits of information extracted from the captive to entice the knocker to enter. Meanwhile Inarra muttered reports of a couple of scouts outside. She was apparently communing with her belligerent crystalline construct who was acting as a spy outside the door.

The scouts weren't completely fooled by Kadyel’s ruse and seemed likely to run. Zacxh and Valron slipped out the door and some others followed. After a few stretched moments of half convincing bluffs and tentative movement, the party found themselves in a sort of standoff.

One of the scouts finally broke in a run down a long hallway. The other was overwhelmed by Trynn, Kadyel and Sorn. Zacxh chased at a dead run after the first.

He caught up with the fleeing scout, but at his frantic pace was unable to get an effective sword stroke in to down him. They passed an intersection and Zacxh heard poorly aimed bolts whiz past as he ran. This was defiantly leading to an ambush, but instead of shrewdly turning back, Zacxh willed his skin to harden and his mind to retreat into its mental fortress. His pace did not slow but changed. His body moved with a supernatural precision and he viewed the ensuing melee from inside his mind with dispassion.

Indeed it was an ambush, but Zacxh heard the pursuing footfalls of his comrades and just dove on in. He faced down four green troops for the few moments it took Valron to come up and help him. Captain Xulleyl joined him as well, and Zacxh knew the guards were doomed. Blood flew and bolts chattered by. battle was joined all around, and though Zacxh did not chop down these troops as quickly as he would like, The captain and Valron did, and Zacxh was able to engage and hold off four or six without injury. He even managed to deal a nasty slash here and there.

Though he was a fury of spinning parries and turned blades, Zacxh felt his defensive stance was not good enough. He cursed his clumsy swordplay and promised himself more training. Something inside him screamed for blood.

Soon all the archers and underlings were dead, but next Zacxh faced down a formidible 'sergeant' who put him on his heels with a pair of wicked slashes to his thighs. The wounds were brutal and tore Zacxh out of his mental reprieve. Luckily Several of Captain Xulleyl’s arrows dropped the sergeant and Zacxh had a moments breathing space to regain his composure.

He took off at a run down a narrow corridor past a tentative few of his comrades in pursuit of the onlt remaining opponent. Only Captain Xulleyl advanced with him. The rest of the party must have spotted the enemy Captain and were acting with much caution. Sorn reported that the enemy had 'vanished.' Though sure to be ambushed by the invisible warrior, Zacxh trusted himself to hold his enemy at bay until he could be reinforced, so he charged down the hallway with a grin.

He burst into a chamber and past Captain Xulleyl. It appeared empty. He held his breath and rotated slowly with his sword held wide. He strained his ears for any sound which might betray an invisible presence.

Behind him he heard the wet ugly sound of a sword plunged into flesh and spun to find a large heavily armed elf pulling his rapier free of Captain Xulleyl's chest. Zacxh snarled, yanked free the masterful dagger he had so recently acquired from his boot and hurled it at the Enemy captain. It was a good throw but was deflected by the warriors exquisite chain mail. Zacxh raised his large blade to charge, but was knocked flat by a terrifying explosion. The damage was devastating. He was burnt terribly, his stubbly hair charred and melted to his skull, and he gaped like a grounded fish for a moment trying to regain his breath. His clothing was burnt away leaving only smiking tatters behind.

Zacxh could see that Captain Xulleyl was in bad shape and that the next moments were critical. He could not afford the time to steel his body and mind for his charge so he just plunged himself in. The next few moments were a blur of pain smoke and clumsily fought battle.
