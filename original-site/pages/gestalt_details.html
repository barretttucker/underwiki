gestalt details

To make a 1st level Gestalt character, choose two standard D&D classes (also referred to as Core classes). You can also choose any of the variant Core classes available in the campaign. Build your character according to the guidelines below.

Hit Dice

Choose the larger Hit Die. For example, a monk/sorcerer would use d8 as her hit die.

Base Attack Bonus

Choose the better progression from the two classes.

Base Saving Throw Bonuses

For each save bonus (i.e. Fort, Ref, Will), choose the better progression from the two classes. For example, a 1st level gestalt fighter/wizard would have base saving throw bonuses of Fort +2, Ref +0, Will +2 -- taking the good Fort save from the fighter class and the good Will save from the wizard class.

Class Skills

Take the number of skill points gained per level from whichever class grants more skill points, and consider any skill on either skill list as a class skill for the gestalt character. Do not add up the amount of skill points gained from both classes, just take the higher amount. For example, a gestalt barbarian/bard would gain skill points per level equal to 6 + INT mod (and of course have four times this amount at 1st level), and can purchase skills from both the barbarian and bard lists as class skills.

Class Features

A gestalt character gains the class features of both classes. A 1st level gestalt rogue/cleric, for example, gets sneak attack +1d6, trapfinding, ability to cast 1st level cleric spells, and the ability to turn or rebuke undead. Class and ability based restrictions (such as arcane spell failure chance for wizards and a druid's prohibition on wearing metal armor) apply normally to a gestalt character, no matter what the other class is.

Progression

A gestalt character follows a similar procedure when he attains 2nd and subsequent character levels. Each time he gains a new level, he chooses two classes, takes the best aspects of each, and applies them to his characteristics. A few caveats apply, however. Class features that two classes share (such as uncanny dodge) accrue at the rate of the faster class. Do not combine or stack them in any way, as you can with similar features from prestige classes. Gestalt characters with more than one spellcasting class keep track of their spells per day seperately. A gestalt character cannot combine two prestige classes at any level, though its okay to combine a prestige class and a core class. And as always, you may select two core classes instead of opting for the use of prestige classes. Gestalt characters must also adhere to the prerequisites needed to take prestige classes, though on the whole they are able to fulfill such prerequisites earlier on in their careers. Pay attention to alignment based features and restrictions when building your gestalt character. For example, combining monk and barbarian would be self-defeating as monks have to be lawful, and barbarians lose their ability to Rage if they become lawful.
