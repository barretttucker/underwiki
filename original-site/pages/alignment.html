alignment

Alignment is typically used in the D&D game to give a starting point for characters, and deliver some help to players in determining their character's motivations, philosophy, beliefs, etc.

Darkelves do not neatly fit into single alignment slots - most of the time they act as 'Lawful Evil', but they will often plan and sometimes act as 'Chaotic Evil'. This is a reliable and consistent factor among all darkelves.

Darkelf morality and psyche have been outlined in the dark elf section of this wiki. Since all of the PC's and the majority of the NPC's have this same base morality, Alignment ceases to be of use for this campaign.

Instead, use the dark elf section and refer to the new rules on virtues and flaws for campaign rules to help generate interesting and varied darkelf characters.

Note that spells and items that use alignment are hereby eliminated; detect good/evil, alignment-based attacks, etc will not be used in this campaign.
