ChapterEight

Chapter Eight: Insanity, Mages and Intrigue, as the quest for a good bottle of wine continues.

He did not have to wait long. He scouted around the main chamber of Nerr’tenden looking for a nice sniper roost. The 'ceiling' of the place reached up well out of sight. hundreds of feet at least. Zacxh wondered where the giant tapering chimney terminated. Someday he would climb up there and explore.

His circular route took him near the post where Anthadax the distracted Oussund mage was pacing furiously. Zacxh paused a moment wondering if he should press his luck by speaking to the mercurial wizard then approached.

'Sir, may I speak?' Zacxh was strangely drawn to this reclusive and extremely powerful elf.

'Do as you like but be quick.' Zacxh covertly looked the wizard over and saw that he was covered in ceremonial scarring. Minor wounds were scabbing all over his arms. This Ossund mage, like so many others who habitually exposed themselves to the arcane, was clearly insane. The elf seemed an unnatural blend of ability and perverse self destructiveness. To watch him was to glimpse a barely functional lunatic, always at the brink of unleashing some horrific and rare force of nature.

Some would argue that arcana, by its very definition was unnatural. Zacxh did not agree. He had seen many 'unnatural' forces manifest them self through the mere power of his concentration. He felt that arcana was merely a word for those special aspects of 'nature' which only revealed them self under rare and special circumstances.

Anthadax had clearly sacrificed large tracts of his soul in the exploration of those circumstances.

'Sir I hope to position myself so as to ambush any attackers which present themselves. Where should I hide so as to avoid being broiled alive by your magics?'

The mage did not look at Zacxh as he spoke, and his voice was disinterested and distracted.

'I will focus on troop concentrations and the most dangerous enemies first. stay away from those.'

Zacxh could see that Anthadax was done speaking and though interested in trying to draw the elf out a bit, he bowed and moved off.

Zacxh circled around a bit more and found a nice little ledge about sixty feet up the wall. He closed his eyes a moment and focused himself, then ran at the wall and impossibly ran right up to the perch. Unnatural...

He pulled out and strung his heavy bow then loosened a few arrows in his quiver. Satisfied all was ready, he crossed his legs sat down to wait. Soon Sorn walked up the wall and sat down next to him. If Zacxh had momentarily bent the laws of gravity and momentum to attain the perch, Sorn openly defied them.

Sorn sat down next to Zacxh at a right angle to the floor and started in.

'You and I haven't spoken since the incident with Kadyel.'

Zacxh immediately grew a bit annoyed at having to again defend his actions and responded coolly.

'Incident? Oh, you must mean when I killed her for being a dangerous and insipid traitor... What is it you would like to say about it?'

Zacxh thought that would kill the conversation, but Sorn pressed on.

'Yeah well I don't miss her, but just because we didn't like her doesn't mean you had the right to kill her. I came here to better my life. I see the Spiderguard as a means of increasing my station and bettering my life. I was happy before I came here and thought this was a place where I could find great opportunity. Now that you killed Kadyel I fear for my life and wonder if I might not be next.'

Zacxh had little patience for this inquisition, but could not help but feel an urge to be understood by this nieve young elf. Perhaps he felt as if Sorn represented part of the conscience which he lost so long ago in the secret fighting arena of house Ossund.

'Look Sorn, I am a practical elf who prefers actions to arguments. Kadyel was a real and pressing threat to not just me but to all of us. You may not like it that I killed her, but someday you will realize that by doing so, I probably saved your life. Or, maybe I am wrong. Either way, I sleep better at night not looking over my shoulder for her next attack, and that alone makes the whole thing worthwhile.'

'You have little enough to fear from me. In fact, if you only knew what an act of absolute loyalty to you my execution of Kadyel was, you would be looking at me as a dear friend not like one of the heads of Valron's mother's whip.'

Sorn was a bit taken aback.

'You use this word friend, and you speak often of loyalty. I am unfamiliar with these things...'

Zacxh let a rare grin surface upon his otherwise empty face.

'Well I have searched into the meaning of these things many years now. Certainly, I am no expert, merely an apprentice practitioner of the art, but I have great confidence that if we are to survive, we will all soon have to show good measures of each. I feel that these ideas are especially critical for warriors and those who rely upon each other for their survival. Without loyalty and friends who were willing to sacrifice for me (as I have sacrificed for you) I would have died a lonely and anonymous death ages ago in the pits. In fact, of all the weapons available to me in a fight, I figure the loyalty of my comrades is by far the most important. That is why I fight so fearlessly in defense of the Spiderguard and *that*, above all else, is ultimately why Kadyel had to die.'

Zacxh could see Sorn working on this idea and decided to back away from the topic to give his young friend time to ponder it.

'So Sorn, How do you find the food here? Personally, I find it horrible. For a while I was overwhelmed by the copious quantities and easy access of the stuff, but of late those aspects of the vile slop only accentuate how truly repulsive it is.'

Sorn looked at Zacxh anew.

'Maybe there is more to you than I thought...'

The discussion turned to food and wine and other fine things, and at last the two elves found some common ground. Zacxh told Sorn of his decision to stop eating anything that failed to nourish the spirit as well as the body. Then asked that the refined elf let him know if he came across anything particularly worth tasting. They also joked about bedding Inarra and other companionable things. Minutes stretched to hours which they passed comfortably.

After awhile, Zacxh decided to get some rest while waiting for the fight to begin. He forced his mind to bend its perceptions of time and space and ran down the wall. Sorn only smiled at how difficult Zacxh made it look. At the bottom Zacxh folded himself up and without ceremony, drifted into a restful trance.

He started to alertness several hours later. Nothing moved, yet something was different. He felt refreshed as he stretched He must have dozed for several hours. Soon a messenger ran through the front gait toward the armory. Zacxh stirred his rested body to its feet and soon Captain Xulleyl emerged walking toward him.

'It begins' she said 'Go get the others.'

Zacxh nodded to his Captain then ran full speed to the common room, unconsciously sprinting up walls to speed his navigation of corners. Walls became floors and low ceilings which he ducked and dodged in his mad flight. His body came more and more alive with each moment of his ballistic exertion. By the time he came skidding to the door of the barracks, he was limbered up and again ready for the coming battle.

He stepped in quickly told Nal and Valron, who happened to be by the door to the commons, that the fight was about to start. Without pause for questions, Zacxh spun on his heel returned at a sprint to Sorn and the sniper's roost.

Zacxh strung his new bow and admired its craftsmanship. Few would have the strength to fully draw the stout weapon, Sorn might not be able to bend it at all, but Zacxh knew it was well matched to his strength and though difficult and tiring to wield, it would provide impressive results. He notched an arrow and waited.

Four strangers entered bearing a dead elf on a stretcher. They set it down and looked about nervously. Then one spoke.

'This Ossund spy was caught amongst the court of house Orlylyl. He bears the mark and manner of a Nerr’tenden spy. House Orlylyl hereby declares war!'

Zacxh was taken aback by the proclamation a moment. What spy did Xulleyl have who might be caught? Why announce the attack so, and what was it about this spy that was so offensive as to precipitate war. Suddenly is was clear. The lowly looking servants were more than mere messengers. They were trusted scouts, probably quite skilled, who came to assess the state of affairs here inside Nerr’tenden before beginning the raid.

The Orlylyl speaker reached into his pocket and drew out a small orb. Zacxh knew he should fire and kill the crier before he could deploy the thing, but almost as if entranced, he watched. The scout tossed the orb into the air where it floated up and split into four with a strange pop. The faintly glowing balls began to hum and just as Zacxh instinctively winced, they let out a horribly bright and blinding flash. Each orb started flashing and humming. The great chamber became a painfully brilliant, strobing scene of chaos. Fighters, both Ossund and Orlylyl appeared on all quadrants and engaged throughout the room.

Zacxh spied the crier enchanting powerful entanglement spells. Zacxh took aim and fired an arrow at him. He dared not look too long through his squinted eyes to see the effect of his shot but he did hear a satisfying meaty thud and a bit off cry of pain as the arrow found its mark. Sadly, the cry did not sound fatal, so Zacxh fired again. Another cry, though the meat cleaving sound of the arrow slamming home was drowned out by the whoosh of a strange fire spell detonating near the great room entrance.

Zacxh squinted over and saw that everything within thirty paces or so of the entrance was on fire. The spy body, the stone, several combatants, all of it. Zacxh fired at another combatant, but a strobe of light thwarted his view of the arrows effect and caused his vision to become little more than one great orange spot. He heard the arrow strike stone.

'Damn those lights, I can barely see!'

Nearby, Sorn was faring worse. The levitating elf curled himself into a defensive ball and clutched his head in his hands.

'Zacxh what is happening, I can't see a thing. I am blinded...'

Zacxh grinned at the blinking elf and drew another arrow.

'We are safe enough here young warrior. Give it a few moments and I believe your eyes will recover. I will stay nearby until you can see.'

Zacxh spotted another target surging and lurching in strobe lit motion. He squinted his eyes nearly closed to avoid the painful flashing light and aimed toward the center of his targets blurry motions. His arrow was surreally illuminated in flight by two nearly simultaneous flashes and in the following moment he watched a wizard lurch under the impact of the shot. The arrow pinned the dying mage's elbow to his ribcage and the Orlylyl wizard collapsed with a gurgled cry of pain.

Zacxh saw Trynn and Valron moving about below him and it looked as if Valron had fatally skewered at least one foe and stood boldly awaiting another. Valron's rigid stance and sweeping trident motions made it clear that he was as blind as Sorn. Zacxh looked too long at his friend and leader and forgot to squeeze his eyes shut at the sound of an impending 'pop' of light. He was dazzled a moment. Almost blinded. The heroic pose of Valron brandishing his trident at unseen foes momentarily hovered in his retinas as a glowing wavering orange and blue shifting afterimage.

Zacxh drew an arrow, heaved back with all his might and fired blindly with his eyes squeezed tight toward the main gate. He thought he had seen motion there a moment ago. He strained his hearing for a meaty sounding impact but perceived nothing in the riotous chaos. He froze a moment in mid draw to throw open some of the doors of his mental fortress. He felt his body stiffen as the demons of his past began to take hold of his physical form. He felt the cold detachment return, and fearing for his sanity lest he end up like Anthadax, quickly took refuge deep within himself, far away from anyplace he was likely to encounter the horrors which began to gleefully run the corridors of his mind.

He fired his bow again, only slightly noticing that it felt like the toy bow of a child in his rage powered arms. The arrow flew toward the main gates and he was sure he hared a grunt of pain about the time it should have found a mark.

Just then a powerful female voice, undoubtedly that of a priestess, rang forth from the gate.

'APPROACH!'

Somehow Zacxh knew instinctively the command was meant for someone else. He looked about and saw Captain Xulleyl running mindlessly toward the voice.

'Sorn, you are okay here. Stay put, and I will come back for you in a minute.'

Sorn blinked again. His vision seemed to be improving. 'No I'm okay. You go. I will fend for myself.'

Zacxh dropped his bow safe on the tiny perch and half ran, half fell down the wall into the melee below. His body was full under the sway of inner horrors now and he found himself drawing his blade and charging into the flames toward the source of that dreadful voice. He knew he would not survive long in the inferno of Anthadax's fire spell, but though he smelled his flesh and clothing burn, as yet he felt no pain.

Deep inside his mental fortress, secluded from the pain and immediacy of the battle, Zacxh wondered if Anthadax's firestorm was a dryly humored response to Zacxh's comment about being broiled alive. He doubted it, the mage seemed too distracted and self absorbed to give much though or care to anything said by a former slave. Still...

The fires raged much hotter than Zacxh had imagined possible. His scars stood up purple and angry and seemed to harden against the fire. It was not enough.

A few more strides and he drew up beside Trynn, Xulleyl, and a large and terrifying Priestess. Behind her were a dozen or so of what Zacxh assumed to be the warrior elite of Orlylyl. Xulleyl and an enlarged Trynn were dancing and lunging around the priestess. They did not appear to be having much success.

Zacxh decided to attack one of the Warriors. The heavily armed elf seemed surprised to see Zacxh emerge from the bright flames and greasy smoke and even more surprised when Zacxh's sword plunged toward his heart. The Warrior twisted painfully away reducing the effect of Zacxh's stroke, but was none the less badly wounded. He panicked and tried to wheel away and Zacxh swiped at him again laying open the flesh covering his left leg.

Zacxh turned from the fleeing fighter to face off against the terrifying Priestess. She radiated power and rage, and her presence on the battlefield was nearly overwhelming. She must have been wreathed in some sort of enchantment. He fought back an uncharacteristic surge of panic at the sight of her. The flames were beginning to overwhelm his flesh and he knew he could not spend much more time within the inferno Anthadax had wrought. If only Trynn could pin her a bit, Zacxh might be able to slip his blade into the Lolth Daughter's neck and silence her dire tongue as he had so recently silenced Kadyel’s.

Trynn groped his huge arms at her again and Xulleyl plunged a dagger into her side but Trynn's grasp was dodged even as a snake headed whip bit into his flesh. Trynn wailed in horror, then turned and fled.

Zacxh understood. He had faced those whips time and again. The venom of them caused panic and pain. Zacxh had tried to flee in terror before their wrath many times himself.

Zacxh assessed the situation. Xulleyl had obviously regained herself and seemed unaffected by the flames. His rescue of the Captain unnecessary, It was time for Zacxh to go. His chance to chop out the heart of the vile priestess would have to wait. He would fall back from this losing tactic and figure out a new vector of attack. Maybe the wizard would soften the Lolth bitch up a bit before Zacxh reengaged.

Valron's matron looked Zacxh square in the eye and issued another command.

'FLEE!' It was not a request.

Zacxh nearly laughed at the nerve of that witch to issue him orders when the enchantment took hold. At first Zacxh felt at though he was being motivated by one of the many demons he had released within his mind. Then he understood. He was bewitched.

His body was running at a full sprint directly away from the Priestess of Lolth. One of the ghosts rampaging about Zacxh's battle giddy mind found something tremendously funny about the whole situation.

He laughed demonically as he ran. 'Mutiny!'

Zacxh cleared the flames and drew up to the far wall of the cavern when the madness passed. He filled with hate and looked around.

Trynn stood nearby looking terrorized.

Anthadax had apparently vaporized several foes with some sort of acrid smelling energy and had caused others to simply detonate into bloody, stumpy, wiggling piles of gore. Zacxh watched the twisted mage a moment as he watched over a helpless victim of one of his spells who was writhing in agony and screaming horribly. It was a bit disturbing not to mention very loud. Zacxh walked up with a sword to deliver a death stroke to the wracked wizard but Anthadax stopped him.

'Leave him.' Again, not a request.

Zacxh shrugged and turned toward the flames. He steeled himself for another charge when Xulleyl and Valron's matron emerged. It appeared they had reached some sort of a truce.

Valron strode up indignant and started demanding explanations. He told of the dead and marked spy that the Spiderguard had found and suggested that a joint Ossund and Orlylyl investigation uncover who was trying to turn the two houses against each other.

Politics and intrigue. Zacxh quickly lost interest. Let the women and nobles sort this out amongst themselves. Zacxh would involve himself in practical matters.

He turned his attention to salvaging equipment and piling it into neat stacks. He wandered about the blood strewn and smoking battlefield picking over the bodies of the dead and keeping a watch on the Orlylyl warriors who were still hovering just outside the main gates.

He finished his salvage all the while serenaded by the anguished screams of Anthadax’s tormented victim then walked over and stood square in the middle of the entrance to face off Orlylyl's warrior detachment. Several Orlylyl sneered at the Idea of Zacxh standing a solo watch against their many. The soldier nursing his nasty chest and leg wound was not one of those. Zacxh stared each down coolly with his dripping sword held loosely in practiced hands. Soon the sneers stopped and Zacxh felt their confidence waver.

Lt. Ardul arrived after a time with his guard. Zacxh was struck as always by the faith Xulleyl placed in her suspicious lieutenant and without a word, he surrendered his vigil to wander toward the bath house. Valron, Nal and Xulleyl would doubtless be busy with their guest awhile and Zacxh had weapons to clean. His skin was tender from flames that would have killed any of those currently standing watch at the gate and he could use a soak.

Zacxh pitied the rest of the Spiderguard their interest in the two faced lies and bluffs which were about to transpire between houses Orlylyl and Ossund. Long experience with priestesses had taught him there would be nothing but poison and death in her words. Zacxh nearly thanked Lolth he was spared the audience, then realized that Lolth had nothing to do with it, or at least nothing to do with him. Zacxh was an elf without faith in the bloated spider queen. He knew she only provided agony and suffering. Never strength, at least not to the likes of himself. The elves of the Underdark had bent to her will for countless millennia. She was the source of their power but also of their limitations. Zacxh seethed in hatred of the Spider Queen a moment as he had so often before then noticed Anthadax still tormenting his screaming pet.

Zacxh took in the scene anew. Anthadax's bleeding arms and gore laced nails suddenly failed to impress Zacxh. Here was just another insane victim of The Spider Queen. Despite all his arcane power, he had lost himself. Too driven for power. Motivated only to achieve greater influence and security in this poisonous twisted world, the poor foolish noble had driven himself mad.

Zacxh grimaced. Such a waste. All that potential squandered when Anthadax could have lived as a king. The sadistic mage could have used his noble status and tremendous arcane power to actually enjoy his life. Instead there he stood mindlessly extracting horror and anguish from some helpless wretch. Did he even hear the screams? Did the sight of all that misery stir some memory of what it was once like to feel?

As he quietly strode past the scene toward the bath house, Anthadax's twisted state deeply touched Zacxh's sense of tragedy.

There was much wrong with the world that no amount of wine would dull, and no bar of soap would cleanse. The only cure lay somewhere within himself. Zacxh felt it with rare certainty. The question was where or what or how.

Only the insane could be oblivious to the insanity of their world. Maybe Anthadax had it right after all. Perhaps the only path to happiness lay in letting mind and conscious destroy itself. Zacxh resolved to meditate on that further while soaking.

Anthadax had his obsessions and Zacxh had his own. At least Zacxh's left him smelling nice. Perhaps tomorrow he would enlist Sorn's help in finding a decent bottle of wine.
