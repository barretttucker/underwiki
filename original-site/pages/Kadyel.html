Kadyel

Starring Blake Tucker as Kadyel Stormbrewer

[kadyel.png]

She died for what she believed. For that she is blessed.

Kadyel Stormbrewer came from a family surrounded with suspicions and doubts about their lives. Dark elves live a secret and unknown existence for the most part, full of plotting, scheming, and self-interest. Her family taught her as a youngster that there might be more. Childhood stories of a fantasy world often enthralled her---stories where people worked together under a common goal without killing each other when disagreements inevitably flowered. Perhaps it was the strange powers her family had that made her such a daydreamer as a child; no one spoke about them of course, and they skipped a generation anyway so were easy to overlook. But her grandmother had a way with things---she had an unnatural ability to start cookfires, tend to the sick, and her soups could never be replicated even when she wrote down every ingredient and her students followed her every instruction. But when Kadyel came of age, she was able to improve her grandmother's recipes. No one pointed it out, but when the women gathered to eat together, the rapidity of the soup's disappearance was proof enough. This contented Kadyel for a time, until outsiders noticed her talents and adolescence arrived. Then she was exposed to society's suspicions and doubts firsthand.

Kadyel's teenage years were fraught with conflict, self-hatred and sublimation. She attempted to compensate for her difference by becoming a stout warrior amongst her fellow girls. Dark elves are indeed matriarchal, and Kadyel was determined to be the best warrior of her age so that no criticism could befall her. And she did well. A few were stronger than her, but none more agile. However, it was her strategic mind and strong sense of personal command that served her best. Always she was able to steer whatever group she commanded, made moreso by her newfound talent of blending in. With no powers or magic, she at last had found a skill that she was good at, one that was not grown from battle. She was a chameleon. She learned to listen and learn before she spoke, and had a gift for making herself seem like a peer to others. Once she realized she could learn and grow on her own, she began to apply herself more and believe in herself. She made peace with her ancestry, and began to understand how to use her congenital magic to her advantage. She found incredible strength in her newfound blend of prowess, magic, strategy and skill.

Finding her niche in the structure of society, Kadyel created for herself a life of promise as both a guard and a sorceror. It was not without suspicion however. Very few elves were allowed to practice magic. Her family was viewed as barely tolerable, for their strange powers did not conform to the codes of the drow. But never had they betrayed their house, and always did they aid the cause, so they were left alone. And Kadyel was allowed to live her life, hoping that someday those childhood dreams of cooperation and peace would arrive, despite her often violent duties as a soldier.

On the morning of her invitation to the Spiderguard, her grandmother had spoken to her. She said she would tell Kadyel more about their line, and where their destiny lay. But that conversation never occurred because of her abduction.

So what did Kadyel believe? She believed she was destined for something. Something different than a normal dark elf life. Something with a ending nearly unheard of. She longed for escape, for an existence that did not involve subterfuge and deceit. She longed to explore other places; to learn about the world outside the warrens of the drow. And she thought she had found that when she discovered her circumstances as member of the Spiderguard. Was this at last her chance to escape? Her chance to bury any doubts and fears about her powers by showing her strength as a leader? She suspected for many years that her family was something other---their very name made then stick out. What is a storm and how does one brew one? To nurture power within this society, power that could force it to change to fit her dreams or escape it once and for all required more than just being a soldier. Likewise, she knew no Matron would listen to her urgings for change or allow her escape if she was merely a member of the Spiderguard. She needed to be leader. She needed to be the public figure of such a group, the star of the cast for such a power to be obeyed.

"Do you surrender all claim to leadership of the Spiderguard, now and forever?" Zacxh had said. She had already surrendered her need to be leader for now. She had destroyed the Spiderguard pin and was ready to bury her need for the moment. But not forever. There was no returning to the life she led, that was clear. There was no more kindly grandmother to divulge her secret past, to clarify those childhood dreams she longed for. Her choice was to give her word and lose all her hopes forever, or to die now and show this bickering crew the value of honor, courage and conviction. To show them that she did not fit in here in this dark place. That she was something other.

For her there was no doubt, nor indeed a glimmer of hope. She knew how this society worked. She understood its need for blood and ascension. So when Zacxh spoke those last words, she accepted her fate gladly, on the point of that sword. She died for what she believed in: her dreams. For that she is blessed.
