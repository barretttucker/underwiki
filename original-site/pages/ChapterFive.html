CHAPTER 5. DINNER

Zacxh pushed his legs to move faster toward the mines despite the ache and complaint of his bloated sloshing belly. He stopped for a moment, sighed then vomited violently. Beautifully prepared fish ejected with great velocity riding on a warm current of delicately spiced mushroom soup of the finest quality. Zacxh spit once to clear his palate.

'Damn shame. That was good soup.'

He pressed into a short sprint to catch his trotting companions. Running was much more comfortable now. He felt foolish to have thought the captain had invited the Spiderguard for an actual dinner, and now having thought about it, it was almost predictable that his beautiful meal was laying in a ditch and he was chasing down another vile enemy of the house.

That was okay with Zacxh. The dinner show had been a bit tedious anyhow. He would rather stretch his legs in pursuit of sport than watch a royal daughter of house Oussund whip her servants to death. The novelty of that sort of show had worn off for Zacxh decades ago.

Trynn had looked so very concerned. It was as if he thought Zacxh might sep up to intervene. Trynn was naive in the way's of Lolth’s chosen. He thought the Priestess might have some feeling about the beating that she was taking some pleasure in it. Zacxh knew better. He knew that the priestess took no more pleasure and gave no more thought to the needless death that Zacxh gave to tying a shoe. It was just a tedious necessary chore. Even the show of fury as she hurled the bludgeoned body off the balcony. Nothing but a show. Like one of Inarra’s fits of sexual ecstasy... just a show.

Zacxh moved his thoughts to the task at hand. His quarry.

...Zacxh crept down the hallway. The weak, and feinting stone shaper who had run from dinner turned out to be some sort of insurgent. He had broken into Captain Xulleyl's quarters to destroy some evidence, and was now holding out deep in the mines.

Already Sorn and Inarra were looking defeated. They motioned to Zacxh the stone shaper mage was further down the passage, and cautioned that it was wielding nasty flying disks. They were nursing some pretty serious looking wounds.

Zacxh pulled up his concealing cloak and crept further along. Valron was right on his heels. It was comforting to have him near.

Zacxh had to smile about the so called 'discipline' of his crew. They had spent the last few weeks drilling on how to take certain formations and assume certain predictable roles in combat. Above all they had resolved to move and to try to act and think as one. In the arena that had all begun to work. In the arena.

Tonight, the Spidergaurd had been 'invited to dinner.' Nominally, they were there to celebrate the cleansing of the mines and to be recognized both by the Steward of Nerr’tenden and by the Stone shapers whose powerful rods had been recovered with the rest of the loot.

When Captain Xulleyl had first extended the 'invitation,' It seemed plausible, if only barely so. Now Zacxh new better. The dinner conversation about the head table, which of course seated all the dignitaries well away from the Spidergaurd, flirted around deceptions about what was recovered and when the rods were to be handed over. In retrospect it was clear that Captain Xulleyl expected some sort of trouble and had the Spiderguard on hand to head it off. Perhaps she had overestimated their abilities.

The Spiderguard all knew their roles and formations and Zacxh was their 'tactical commander.' Then one of the dignitaries departed early and after another course of soup and wine, Inarra started whispering to Sorn. Before Zacxh knew what was happening, half the guard was running for the door and the other half were trying to covertly gain the attention of the captain. Once all was said and done, guard members were spread all over Nerr’tenden and beyond, toward the mines, Captain Xulleyl looked mildly bemused and annoyed and Zacxh knowing little of what was transpiring, only that there was a Xulleyl imposter running around town, was patiently slurping his soup. Ah the soup. Such a waste.

So there he was, creeping up upon what was presumably a powerful and battle ready mage. Zacxh paused a moment and dove deep into the safe recesses of his mind. He awakened old pain and fear to run rampant in the untended parts of his mind. He waited while those old daemons gave strength and resolve to his flesh. after a moment he felt calm and dispassionate but wore an enraged body ready for war. It was a good thing too, for just as he crept around the next corner leading to the old insurgents headquarters, a shuriken slammed into his shoulder with enough force to nearly spin him around. It was like being hit by a club. The wind was knocked out of Zacxh but his infuriated flesh pressed on.

'Look out! Shuriken!' he gasped out while gulping for air.

Zacxh sprinted for cover ahead around the next corner. He pressed against the rocks and used his cloak to melt into the stone. He did not relish the thought of being hammered by more of those amazing missiles.

Across the room, Zacxh could see other guard members moving up to join him in cover. He watched them, feeling secure in his cover when four more shuriken came hurtling toward him. Two hit the rocks, behind which he crouched, but two hit him with the force of hammers. He saw stars and slumped to the floor. His last thought before several moments of senselessness was that the damn disk launching mage was either invisible or able to hurl disks around the corner. Either way, despite armor, thickened skin and mental barriers, the disks had hit him with enough force that they would have killed most members of the guard. Zacxh was much tougher than most, but still the force of the attack was staggering.

This was not going to be a fair fight. Unlike his battles in the pits, Zacxh realized he had the opportunity to withdraw if need be. He did not like that idea. First he would test the water with this 'mage.'

Zacxh refocused his mind. He felt his hurts fall away and the fury within his mind force his bruised body to alertness. He would act as a barrier for his guard, drawing the fire of this frightening foe so that his team could try to find some weakness or advantage. It was not going to be pleasant. Zacxh focused on the image of the bear which was so powerfully in his dreams of late. He specifically focused his mind on that aspect of the bear which made it so resilient, its bulk and stability and sheer force of spirit.

He waited a moment. Then his body began to respond. His loose cut garments began to squeeze and stretch with his increased bulk. His huge sword felt like a toy in his bulging and furry hands.

Normally, he would well overmatch a foe in this state. This time... well, unless Nal or some other spell caster could find a chink in this guy's defense, retreat was fast becoming the option.

Zacxh hoped that perhaps their might be some way to let the mage go and tail him to find out what he was up to here in the mines. He wondered if maybe it was time to let the scouts earn their pay.
