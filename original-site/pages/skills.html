Bathmaster (Wis)

The skills of providing for guests in a bath house involve massage, washing, application of scented unguents and applying restful and healing salves.

A successful DC 10 check allows for improved long-term care, increasing healing by 1 hit point per day. Use of Oil of Relaxation (see Craft Bath Oils Skill) adds +5 to the roll (in addition to their normal effects on a single subject).

A successful DC 15 check provides a healthful experience, reducing fatigue by two levels and removing a number of non-lethal damage equal to the number the skill check exceeded the DC. This effort takes two hours. Use of Oils of Relaxation and Cure Minor Wounds each add +2 to the roll.

A successful DC 20 check provides a very healthful experience, reducing fatigue by two levels and healing one point of regular damage for each point the skill check exceeds the DC. This effort takes two hours. Use of Oils of Relaxation and Cure Light Wounds (normal) each add +2 to the roll.

Attempting to use bathmaster techniques outside of a bath house adds +10 to the DC required.

Craft Bath Oils (Int)

This skill allows one to create non-magical relaxing and healing oils, salves and unguents. Each attempt requires a full day of work. Attempting to create bath oils without appropriate equipment (pots for boiling water, funnels and coils for processing and extracting, etc) adds +15 to the DC required. Materials are expended whether the attempt is successful or not.

Interrogate (WIS)

You are adept at using torture to extract truthful information from unwilling prisoners. Use this skill in place of Bluff, Intimidate and/or Sense Motive when performing torture.

Check: To perform an interrogation, the subject must be secured in a grapple, ordinary restraints, or the bindings of a specialized torture device. The allowed frequency of performing a check varies by situation and implements used, but is usually once per round. Implements of torture typically provide a circumstance bonus to this check. Interrogation of a bound subject requires two phases: torture and questioning, as follows:

  • Torture: The DC to successfully torture a creature is 10 + the creature's level or HD. Damage done during the attempt occurs regardless of the success of this roll.

    Questioning: Once a creature is successfully tortured, you can attempt to pry the answer to one question from them. They get a Will save to resist; the DC is the skill check rolled to torture them. Successful saves require a new torture attempt before a question can be asked again. Victims get a cumulative +1 bonus to this save for each successful save they make against torture with a given implement. Any bonuses accrued don't apply to resist an implement with a higher circumstance bonus.

A torture subject may attempt to mislead the torturer during interrogation. The victim's Bluff check is opposed by the torturer's Interrogate check, which takes a -3 circumstance penalty due to the torturer's belief in the effectiveness of his methods.

If brought to 0 or fewer hit points by a device during the course of torture and subsequently healed, a torture victim doubles the circumstance bonus on the same device in further torture.

A successful Interrogate check vs. the victim's Sense Motive allows half the circumstance bonus from a torture device by threatening the subject with its use.

Try Again: Unlike normal methods of extracting information, a torturer may repeat the Interrogate check to extract information repeatedly.

Synergy: If you have 5 or more ranks in Bluff, Intimidate, or Sense Motive, each grants a +2 bonus on Interrogate checks.
