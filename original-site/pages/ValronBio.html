ValronBio

One-hundred and forty-one years ago, Valrón’dar was born to a Cleric mother, Qualn’dril, and metal worker father, Tluth’ididyrr in the house of Orlylyl. Not being female, Valrón learned silver smithing from his father and watched younger sister, Lael’Thara train to be a cleric under his mother’s doting tutelage. Feeling the pressure to live up to her mother’s high standards, Lael deceptively uses Valrón to practice her skills. Lael swears Valrón to secrecy that he is helping her. After years of helping his sister study, Valrón becomes relatively skilled in the ways of a Cleric and grows resentful that he will never become a priest in his house. In finding an outlet for his pent up frustration, Valrón becomes an excellent fighter and is posted to the house Guard when he comes of age.

One day, Lael is given the robes of a full priestess, even though Valrón knows she could not have possibly passed her test. Valrón loses it, curses his mother and the Spider Goddess, and storms off in a rage. Valrón travels a short distance away from the city, and levitates up to his secret sanctuary. He centers himself, and for the first time prays for guidance, but not to Lolth.

A strange man suddenly appears before him, introducing himself as Nal’dax. Nal’dax tells Valrón that if he prays to Rauvdril then he will help him become a full Cleric. Nal’dax tells Valrón that there is a secret movement afoot to quell the powers of Lolth by bringing in Rauvdril. Nal’dax’s job is to seek out those with the potential to learn the ways of a Cleric and who also have the charisma and talent necessary to begin spreading his word without being found out and killed.

Nal’dax tells Valrón that he must train on his own under the guidance of Rauvdril and the domains of Strength and Travel. When he has become accomplished in the ways of Teleportation and Righteous Might (the 5th order) he will be visited again by Nal’dax to be taken to meet others like him in the Secret Society of Rauvdril. Nal’dax tells Valrón that just as he trains in fighting, he must also equally train himself in ways of the Cleric. But he must be cunning and not allow others to find out what he is doing.

Happy to finally have found some place in the world, Valrón sets off back to House Orlylyl only to find a war afoot between Orlylyl and Oussund. He learns that Oussund has attacked Orlylyl because Orlylyl owns the only other sapphire mine, Gaullidurth, which is better located than Oussund’s mine at Nerr’tenden. Valrón enters the fight on behalf of his House but is captured by Oussund. Oussund makes a ransom offer to Orlylyl for Valrón’s safe return but Orlylyl refuses to take Valrón back, telling Oussund to just kill him because of Valrón’s disrespect to his House and to Lolth.

Oussund instead decides to keep Valrón alive because of his exceptional talent as a fighter and future political value. If any other house decided to destroy Orlylyl, Oussund could present Valrón as evidence of a living nobility of Orlylyl. This would cause the victor house to be destroyed as well, thus taking out two houses at once.

Having been left for dead by his House, Valrón joins the Guard of House Oussund, where he begins his self training as fighter and closet cleric. Valrón expects that his house will eventually try to contact him, either to kill him or get him to act on their behalf as a spy in Oussund, or both. One fateful day Valrón eats poisoned porridge for breakfast and keels over.
