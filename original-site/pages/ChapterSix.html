ChapterSix

Zacxh's final Command

For prologue to this chapter see posts 446 to 487 at:

http://games.groups.yahoo.com/group/spiderguard/message/446

Zacxh could see from the look of shock and perhaps a touch of panic on his face that Valron had not picked up on the subtle message Zacxh was trying to send. Perhaps Zacxh's message had been too subtly hidden in double meaning. That was dangerous.

Zacxh took a breath and momentarily collected his thoughts. He thought he had a captive audience, and hoped to make enough of an impact with his next few words to at last consolidate the Spiderguard into an effective unit. Perhaps he would at last bring Kadyel into the fold and end the infighting. Perhaps he would finally relieve himself of a command he could not hope to hold. Perhaps he could finally stop looking over his shoulder.

Perhaps.

He hoped that in the next few moments he would not be bathing in blood.

The plan was risky and hinged on a lot of only loosely held assumptions.

Valron and Trynn were Zacxh's two biggest concerns. Valron because he had made such an unreasonable and domineering bid for command, and Trynn because he was utterly conflicted in his loyalties.

Ironically the uncertainty in their support was the very reason Zacxh could wait no longer. The clock had run out on his opportunity to deal with the only remaining obstacle to a functional Spiderguard.

Kadyel.

Wait any longer and Valron would usurp Zacxh's authority. Delay, and Trynn would forget his vow to follow this next and final command 'without question.'

Zacxh's brief pause to collect all those thoughts was interrupted, first by a misled Nal who incredulously asked if Zacxh was foolish enough to think he could kill all forty of the elves present, and then by Kadyel who may have caught an intuitive glimpse of what was to come.

Kadyel quickly and in her melodramatic way tossed yet more incitements of Zacxh's failing leadership and made a great show of renouncing her 'rightful' claim to lead the Spiderguard by smashing the phony golden pin she had only moments before thrown on the ground. This unconvincing, conciliatory drama was punctuated with a little tear on the Wizard's cheek.

Zacxh's face remained impassive as he laughed within his mental fortress. The Wizard's actions only confirmed that her pin was just another in a long string of parlor tricks designed to undermine Zacxh's flagging authority.

He decided to act swiftly and with as much impact as possible. With luck this would be his last deed as commander of the Spiderguard and he hoped to place Valron decisively in command.

Zacxh took a last breath to steel his resolve. All eyes were upon him, and the stage was set as well as he could hope. He began his gambit.

'Kadyel, you have proven time and again to be an obstacle to our evolution into effective unit!'

Zacxh put as much force into his words as his rage motivated body would allow. Still, he wondered if his passionless eyes would convey the severity of his words.

'This can continue no more! So, you will swear three oaths to the Spiderguard here and now. If you refuse or waver in any way Trynn and I are going to kill you where you stand.'

Zacxh risked a look to Trynn to gauge his level of support and was deeply concerned by the conflicted look the spineless monk suddenly wore. He resolved himself to continue on and hope Kadyel didn't call what was rapidly and unintentionally turning into either a bluff or a pitched solo battle.

'First!'

'Do you swear on pain of death to now and forevermore renounce any claim to command of the Spiderguard?'

Zacxh forced himself to remain deeply hidden within his mind. He could not allow any bit of his anxiety to bleed through to his body lest he self defeat any chance of success.

Kadyel blinked and looked taken aback. She was obviously caught off guard and a bit confused.

Zacxh immediately saw his error. He had asked the hardest oath first. He had forgotten a basic tenant of coercion, get them talking and agreeing before you slip in the whammy. Crap.

Kadyel blinked, incredulous.

'What? you want me to give up *any* claim to lead the Spiderguard'

Zacxh pressed on. Fully committed.

'Yes Kadyel. Swear it now or die.'

She paused only a moment, then committed suicide.

'I refuse.' She said.

The silence of the chamber was deafening. No one dared move or even breathe as the moment stretched. Disaster hung in the air like a specter and it seemed somehow a violation of the moment to so much as twitch.

Zacxh felt his heart beat once... twice... then roared into the attack.

The chamber exploded into activity. The next few moments were a blur of clumsily launched grapples and attacks as Zacxh was besieged on all sides by every one of the forty elves in the cavern who could get to him.

Inarra attacked his mind with some alien mental force, probably trying to steal his resolve. She failed.

Trynn enlarged to titanic proportions and attempted to restrain him... and failed.

Nal's thirty some minions attacked en masse swinging their short swords furiously all about the dodging leaping warrior. Their blades glanced off his iron skin and phantasmal barriers like pebbles off a stone wall. Collectively, they succeeded only in pissing Zacxh off.

Valron lashed out and missed.

Sorn sent dark eldritch energies which went unnoticed by Zacxh.

In short, he was opposed by every elf that could in any way intervene, but in Zacxh's highly attuned and furious state, they all failed to stop him. All but one.

Kadyel just stood and watched as Zacxh placed his sword to her throat. She apparently decided that she could not hope to oppose him and played her final mind game.

Zacxh indeed paused, if only for a fraction of a second while his resolve wavered. His warriors mind and pit fighting instinct had a firm hold on him. Yet still he hesitated.

There was nothing for it. Zacxh retreated into his mental fortress and flung open all the doors of his internal terror. His arms bunched, and he brutally rammed his huge blade home.

Kadyel erupted. She became a volcano of blood and staggered back fatally wounded.

Wounded but not dead. She gurgled up something spiteful as she wheeled back only to be knocked flat by Trynn's enlarged fists. Zacxh quickly concluded that Trynn was trying to protect her by taking her out with a stunning blow. Perhaps he thought Zacxh was careless enough to not ensure she was finished.

Zacxh had been around plenty of death. Plenty enough to know the wizard was still clinging to a spark of life. He leapt over Trynn's huge, grasping arms and spun to drive his blade again into the sprawled and gushing Kadyel. This time his blade struck bone and continued on. She was not *technically* decapitated, but was without a doubt dead.

Zacxh, having completed the critical portion of his task, began phase two.

It was time for him to figure out how to survive this thing.

Judging by their actions, not a single elf of the Spiderguard had supported his action, and in fact were now all actively trying to kill him.

He could feel Inarra digging around in his skull looking for a soft spot, and she was getting perilously close to finding one. Nal's thirty minions were moving in and Valron and Trynn looked out for blood.

Zacxh calculated quickly. He had no desire to fight any of them, but his pit instincts gave him the numbers anyhow. Nal, the thirty minions, Sorn, Inarra. He could take them all given a fair share of luck and their typical disjointed tactics. But Trynn and Valron? Too many. Not to mention Inarra’s intrusion into his skull. She would have to die first if he decided to fight. His only choices were to run or yield and wonder if he would be given a chance to justify himself before a summary execution.

Zacxh stopped and found himself clinging to the wall just a foot or so from Sorn. The lad looked terrified and let loose with another ineffective blast of dark eldritch power. The fury of his attack on Kadyel had torn him out of his mental fortress, and Zacxh couldn't help but smile.

'So Sorn, where do you stand on this whole traitor issue?'

Sorn was too shaken to speak.

'Are you done Zacxh?' Nal called out from his personal army perhaps wondering if Zacxh was still contemplating their collective death.

Bloodlust, adrenalin and a dozen other energies only describable to the psionically gifted warred with Zacxh's common sense. He was as yet untouched by the combat and indeed entertained the thought of slacking his anger on a dozen or so of Nal's conscripts.

He came to his senses and hopped to the ground to call out.

'I am if you are Nal.'

Nal muttered some command over his shoulder and the conscripts backed off. Zacxh walked up to Kadyel’s very dead remains and used her cloths to wipe the blood off his blade. He then casually stowed the exquisite sword over his shoulder and crossed his arms in an authoritative pose. After all, the Spiderguard was his to command and in his mind he had just drastically improved its prospects. He was proud of his work.

He stood his ground listening as the debate over his future began when suddenly Inarra found him. He must have unconsciously let down his guard for dark clouds passed by his vision and his muscles locked in place. Inarra had targeted and shut down some critical function of Zacxh's brain and he was rendered immobile. He struggled to move, perhaps to speak, but it was hopeless. She had him.

He could only vaguely see or hear what transpired next and in his strange physiologic state, could scarcely bring himself to care. Instead, he wandered the empty hallways of his terrible mind and ushered a myriad of distasteful memories and horrors from his past back into their neat little nooks and crannies. Then he collected his remaining sanity and retreated to his mental fortress to wait.

He had the vague impression that Sorn was prattling on about something and heard Inarra cooing annoyances into his ears. Nal chimed in as did Valron with an outraged tone.

After a few moments It occurred to Zacxh that if someone wanted to avenge the death of the universally despised wizard they would have already done it, so he just settled his mind into Inarra's hold and relaxed. If she wanted to dig around in his head then she was welcome. There were no secrets to be found there other than secret horror. He welcomed her investigation knowing that if she continued to probe, she would only find such visions as to never intrude again. He mused about weather Inarra would be as philosophical if it were she who were frozen.

Eventually she let go. Perhaps the lack of resistance to her prodding had become tedious. Zacxh concluded as much, and further that all females were essentially alike in their love of making males squeal. She would get none of that from him, only horror. And she would have to prod to find even that.

Zacxh looked down and found that he had been locked up with Nal's manacles. While Zacxh wondered if they could hold him, Nal approached to reclaim them.

Zacxh gave assurances that the restraints were not necessary, then briefly explained that as Guard leader it was his prerogative to improve the unit through any means necessary. The Spiderguard, though not happy, seemed to accept his explanation.

Zacxh suspected that they would. Some because they disliked Kadyel as much as he, some because Zacxh was indeed in charge and some ultimately because Zacxh immediately nominated Valron the new leader and saw her unanimously confirmed within a span of about two minutes.

It was Valron's confirmed leadership which Zacxh had been after all along. Kadyel would never have accepted an order from anyone, and thus never have allowed the Spiderguard to evolve into anything more than a conflicted squabbling mob. Zacxh let go a contented sigh, and at length stood his watch. Sorn, strangely, volunteered to join him.

Valron continued to petulantly spit out meaningless orders just to overtly assert the dominance which Zacxh had presented him upon a gilded platter. Zacxh bore up under it realizing that his former friend would likely realize soon enough what a tremendous service he had been provided. Besides the ever more self concerned noble was so distracted by his quest to save his own skin, that he would likely become too lost in the more pressing concerns of command to continue the tirade. So, relieved of an immediate threat to his life, Zacxh reflected upon his decision.

He had almost completely misjudged the effect his action would have upon the guard, and was unsure whether this was an indictment of his character or theirs.

Instead of respecting his command prerogative and the wisdom of his action, they were all acting as if Zacxh had somehow lost his mind or was acting out of petty hatred of that damn wizard.

Kadyel had been a thorn in the side of the Spiderguard from the first. She had lied repeatedly in many small attempts to undermine Zacxh's unwanted command. She had tried to steal the command through several laughable presentations of jewelry, and when Zacxh had called her on the most recent one, she smashed it in an attempt to cover her blaring misread of the impact it would have. She either intentionally, or through gross incompetence, used her self proclaimed mastery of magic to all but kill not only Zacxh, whose command she was overtly covetous of, but nearly Captain Xulleyl as well.

She was annoying it is true and she poked her nose, uninvited, into many of the discussions Zacxh was having with other Guard members, but it was the underlying motivation for those intrusions which Zacxh found unbearable. That is, she used every opportunity available to disagree with and undermine Zacxh's position on everything from matters of honor and what makes a good leader, to what lace to put atop the other when tying a boot. Zacxh firmly believed that she spun these disagreements merely to insinuate that Zacxh could not lead, and if she were still alive, would now be using a similar tactic to give Valron's command the death of a million pin pricks.

The worst problem of all was one Zacxh had discovered on the first day and had confirmed twice since. Kadyel refused to swear an oath of allegiance to the Spiderguard. Zacxh asked her to join him that first day by swearing allegiance to the guard and she refused saying she would commit nothing to the unit. He asked her again weeks later after the group had settled into a routine and again she refused. And even when Nal and others demanded and end to the squabbling so as to focus on the mission she still refused unity.

Zacxh concluded that he made the right choice. True he had been angry. True and had drawn his sword only moments after coming to his epiphany. But his decision had been brewing a long time. Now it was time to sit back and see how the Spiderguard reacted. Only thus could he decide if his gambit had paid off. At least he would know quickly. Either he had saved the guard, or he had condemned it to rapid decline.

Time would tell.

The guard quickly regrouped and settled into the long march toward Nerr’tenden. Zacxh took up his customary position in the marching order and after only a brief pause for questioning and a cursory harassment delay, they passed uneventfully through????.

The conscripts were posted under watch in Nerr’tenden main chamber and the Spiderguard reported directly to Captain Xulleyl in her chambers.

Zacxh requested the honor of introducing Valron to the Captain as Sergeant and she merely quirked an eyebrow at the decision.

Xulleyl asked for an explanation as to what happened to Kadyel who Zacxh was carrying over his shoulder and he briefly replied that she was a divisive and insidious traitor and that he had killed her for it. The captain muttered something about Lolth helping those who help themselves and dismissed the topic without concern. Trynn and a few others pointed out that they had tried to stop Zacxh, but the Captain seemed disinterested in further detail.

Valron proceeded to give a full accounting of the events of the journey. Here and there someone or another would add in a missed detail and the Captain asked a few leading questions. At length, and to general ascent, the captain asked the question which put the guard at ease.

'So you are sure that Illium knows it was you who killed him? Good. Now maybe that bastard will take me seriously.'

The Spiderguard collectively gave a sigh of relief. The speculation over weather they had performed the mission to the satisfaction of Captain Xulleyl was answered. there was only one loose end and Nal addressed it.

'I doubt that on his return we will be welcomed to pass through???’

Captain Xulleyl waved a dismissive hand. 'Don't worry too much about that Nal. You will be welcomed well enough. Illium will ensure it. That was indeed a part of our goal.'

'So are there any other questions for me before you get to work mining that mineral?'

Zacxh spoke carefully.

'Sir, before I killed her, Kadyel presented us with this.'

Zacxh held up the many pieces of Kadyel’s shattered gold plated spider pin.

'She said it was a token from you which granted her command of this unit. Then she destroyed it. That seemed unlikely at the time, but I was wondering if you recognized it.'

Xulleyl took the shattered pieces of pin and examined it.

'Interesting. Well it just looks like a gold plated silver pin to me...'

Xulleyl handed the remnants back with a blank look.

'That is what we thought as well sir. Thank you.' Zacxh accepted the seemingly bogus pin and stuffed it into a pouch.

The guard returned to their barracks to get some rest and a meal before beginning the mining operation in the morning. Trynn protested Kadyel’s death yet again to an unsympathetic Zacxh (*see posts*) and despite Zacxh's offer to move out, Valron insisted that they continue to room together. Zacxh agreed, and hoped the arrangement would enhance their common security.
