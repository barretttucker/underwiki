ChapterTen

Heresy

The entire Spiderguard except for Inarra, soon wound up back in the barracks. Zacxh watched the faces of his cohorts carefully, hoping to read their intentions more accurately this time. He fairly bubbled over with enthusiasm for Dinlin's end, but all too clearly remembered how vulnerable and alienated he had made himself the last time he tried something bold within the Spiderguard; Kadyel’s death held an important lesson for Zacxh; don't assume too much with this group. He pulled himself within his mental sanctuary and hid his enthusiasm. This time, he would hold his tongue.

The discussion about Dinlin began almost right away. And as usual Zacxh was surprised by the feelings of his crew. Unbelievably, they were all of the blasphemous opinion that Dinlin must die. They all agreed that it was not only best for the Spiderguard, but the secret desire of Xulleyl and the steward as well. Unanimously, the group quickly decided that it was not a matter of whether she should be eliminated, just a matter of how and when. It was heresy and madness, the whole idea, and yet for reasons that would soon be made clear, everyone was fully onboard.

Zacxh took it all in with pleased enthusiasm. Sorn disliked the Priestess's unjustified and dangerous rage. He also had an interest in protecting the elf who he was convinced might truly be a priest. Sorn had just returned from speaking to the condemned elf and found out that Rorlint came from the same destroyed house that was once his own. The house held very liberal ideas about the roles of males in society and was destroyed because of them, though at whose hands was as usual, a mystery. Sorn felt that whatever means was necessary to save his brother elf was indeed warranted, and as he was so fond of saying... He had no love of Dinlin...

Predictably, Nal had much more pragmatic reasons for Dinlin's removal from power. He felt the Priestess was too unstable and zealous to have around such a small outpost as Nerr’tenden. He seemed to feel she might be a liability to the operations of the unconventional Spiderguard, and so, endorsed her execution with all the passion of a house servant condemning a dead rat to the trash bin.

Trynn listened to the discussions with a fearful yet growing enthusiasm in his eyes. He seemed shocked and frightened by the very idea, but when gently queried, he emphatically agreed in principle, despite misgivings about the guards ability to pull this thing off. He had an understandable slave's fear of retribution, but also clearly remembered and never quite got over the scene at dinner one night where Dinlin savagely beat a slave to death merely for spilling soup. Trynn's motivation was the opposite of Nal's. To the fang of Loth, justice demanded a reckoning.

As Inarra was not present, that left only Valron, who agreed to the plan in an almost distracted manner as if Dinlin's death were a peripheral matter in a larger scheme.

Zacxh could not read his friend, but did not spend much time on the riddle. He was far too enthusiastic to begin planning to give pause to examine Valron's motivations.

All the Spiderguard present agreed it was best to devise a plan and then present it to Inarra. Nal surmised that she was vain and self interested enough to have little care about Dinlin's fate as long as the execution of the plan was good sport and shone her in a positive light. Let her feel powerful and in control, and all agreed she would happily join in on the plot.

So the Spiderguard set upon their first self motivated course of action; a fact lost upon none of them. The air was electric as they began devising their plot.

Some discussion about ambushing the priestess on her way through the catacombs was entertained, but dismissed as being too costly in terms of peripheral damage to the priestess's escort. Other half baked plans were proposed and soon dismissed for a variety of reasons. Slowly the room fell silent and the Guard found itself stumped.

Out of nowhere a plan began to form in Zacxh's mind. He thought about all the superstitious fear felt toward the 'priest'. He envisioned Dinlin's single minded and unreasoning rampages and in his minds eye, he saw all the possible routes of egress from Nerr’tenden. Then like a divine revelation, it hit him.

The plan.

The plan was simple and elegant. it played to Dinlin’s blind rage and to Nerr’tenden’s irrational fears. It took advantage of all the best assets of the Spiderguard and utilized the terrain in a decisive manner. Zacxh loved it, as a thing of beauty, and therefore felt somehow certain that it was doomed to go terribly wrong.

The key to the plan was the potion of invisibility which Zacxh had been hoarding since his promotion to leader. He wondered if the Captain would have been so quick to give it to him had she known how he would use the valuable gift.

The plan called for Zacxh to pick up a magically disguised Sorn, drink the potion, and ferry him invisibly to the stockade where he would set Sorn down and snatch the prisoner up. Effectively one would appear as the other vanished. The 'priest' would appear to have executed a mystical and frightening escape aided by his perhaps not so false god, and in the ensuing fear and chaos, Sorn, escorted by an invisible, heavily laden Zacxh, would lead any pursuers on a mad chase to the mines where they would lose all but Dinlin (who was certain to follow in enraged pursuit) at a precipice which only Zacxh, Sorn and Dinlin would be able to magically or mystically navigate. Zacxh was sure Dinlin would not pause to wait for clumsy male escorts to levitate down the precipice in hot pursuit.

Now, unescorted, Dinlin would come around a corner to be faced by Valron standing ready to receive her charge. In her furious state, she would surely be quick to see the treachery and fly into a rage. Her attack was almost certain, and she would be beset upon by the waiting Spiderguard. She would die alone and mysteriously while in pursuit of what appeared to be a powerful, or at least crafty priest.

The mines were extensive and a great place to dispose of a body. The priest and his pursuer would effectively disappear, and the inevitable papal investigation would be free to come up with whatever conclusions they wished. There would be no witnesses and no evidence leading to Spiderguard... Zacxh hoped.

It was close to a perfect murder. Even so, if it went wrong and Someone recognized a Spiderguard or their handiwork... they were all doomed.

Breakout.

Zacxh crept up to the main gates of Nerr’tenden as if walking on broken glass. One of the crucial points of the plan was that he sneak into Nerr’tenden undetected, and one of Ardul's gate guards was cocking his head and looking suspiciously in Zacxh's general direction. Zacxh risked a few more carefully placed steps and cursed himself for not devising some sort of diversion to get himself more easily through the gate.

Heartbeats passed with nearly palpable tension, then the guard cocked a sardonic eyebrow, chided himself, and returned to a state of watchful disinterest. The danger was past. Zacxh slipped through the gate with as much stealth as possible considering he carried Sorn over his shoulders like a sack of lightly perfumed grain.

The stockade stood ominously in the center of the courtyard. Zacxh had counted on taboo and superstition to keep it's immediate vicinity vacant, and was not disappointed. The backwater elves of Nerr’tenden kept well clear of the heretic in their midst’s.

Zacxh crept up for the most part silently. Undisciplined Sorn could not seem to hold still in his excitement, and yet again shifted his weight slightly causing one or two missteps. Thankfully, no one was close enough to notice the noise.

Zacxh whispered into Rorlint's ear for a moment and the elf answered loudly with an unexpected cry...

'My prayers are answered!'

Horrified by the priest's lack of discretion, Zacxh froze a moment... then grinned. The Priest had just called out for divine intervention. Good enough. The stage was set and it was time to act.

Zacxh kicked open the lockless latch to the stockade and set down Sorn. Just as the lightweight dancer appeared, Zacxh grabbed Rorlint's sagging body and crept invisibly to the side waiting for a reaction.

Sorn Looked a bit panicky in his near exact disguise but nonetheless, dashed toward the main gate as planned. The guards there sent up a riotous alarm and formed a blockade of drawn rapiers and determined looks to stop him. Sorn feinted left then darted right. He placed a foot on the wall and even as a guard swung wildly at him placed a light hand on the heavily armed elf's shoulder and used it as a catapult to fling himself high into the air. Sorn executed a perfect inverted pirouetted with a half twist and landed lightly past the flabbergasted guards. It was beautiful to watch. Zacxh was sure it was a move he would never be able to equal, and affirmed to himself that Sorn was going to prove a very hard priest to catch.

Zacxh quietly murmured to the priest over his shoulder to be silent and flung open some of the internal barriers of his mind. Quickly Zacxh filled with psionic power and plowed through the gates undetected, and with none of Sorn's panache. He began his flight toward the mining outpost with unnatural and demonic speed. Rorlint clung to Zacxh's hard bouncing shoulders desperately, possibly more out of fear at being dropped at this speed than out of any fear of discovery.

It was only a matter of moments before Zacxh and his cargo hauled up behind Sorn's pursuers. They had settled into a fast shuffle close behind the dancer and it had become a sort of sprint race where the pace slowed and unexpectedly quickened at odd moments. Sorn was unencumbered by arms and armor, and with his toned dancers body was having little difficulty staying just one step ahead. He almost convinced even Zacxh when he 'accidentally' cut a turn a hair too sharply and laid a gash open on his leg. As planned, the blood trail would be easy to follow...

Zacxh did not have time to watch the pursuit. He needed to get through the guard post at the mines before his potion wore off. Normally it was a half hours walk to the mine. Zacxh figured he had maybe three or four more minutes. If he didn't make it, the plan would suddenly become much more complicated.

Zacxh saw his chance and bolted past. His vision was red with fury and fear from the daemons which drove his legs at an impossible rate. He knew the stampede had to make some noise, but he did not have time to care. Only in retrospect did he realize that he had run a 'barrel roll' maneuver up and around the ceiling over the pursuing guards. He hoped the running pursuers were too breathless and absorbed in the chase to hear him.

Zacxh was safely in front of the chase (and the news of the escape) so he lengthened his stride and ran like the wind.

'I am speed... I am speed... I am speed!' He panted his windless mantra and pumped his legs. No one ever reached the mine as fast as that, and it was unlikely to ever happen again. He held his breath for the three seconds it took to blow by the guard checkpoint and finally slowed his pace to a walk once safely concealed by a turn down a seldom used and abandoned shaft. He looked down to see his hand fading back into being and set down his disheveled cargo.

'That was too close warrior. I can walk from here...' Rorlint seemed to take the whole thing in stride.

Zacxh was doubled over with fatigue and much too winded to speak but cocked an eyebrow as if to imply that knowledge of the priests ability to self ambulate might have been more helpful about a quarter of a mile earlier.

'Keep moving then, they can't be far behind' Zacxh wanted to hand this elf off to Trynn quickly, and get back to escorting Sorn.

Again, as planned, Zacxh passed custody of Rorlint to Trynn at a hidden spot in the mines and waited for Sorn to run by with his pursuers. Sure enough it was only a minute or two before the winded party straggled into view. Sorn was looking a bit worse for wear from the run and had a small bolt protruding uncomfortably from his upper back. The pursuit had grown. Zacxh had stupidly failed to anticipate that. Some of the guards from the Mine outpost had joined the chase. Now there were ten or so. Too many.

Zacxh looked at Inarra who crouched in the hidey hole with him. She seemed lost in thought. Probably reading the thoughts of the pursuers to judge the effectiveness of the ruse. Zacxh made an adjustment to his view of the environment around him and tried out his newest trick. His skin rippled and changed and colored. Soon he looked like the stone and darkness around him. He became very difficult to see and completely unrecognizable as himself. He dropped out of his concealment.

It only took a moment to catch up with the slower pursuers and he had to follow them closely a while before any noticed him.

'Hey, here he is!... Wait what is that??'

Zacxh darted down a side passage and several of the guards broke off after him. The poor bastards were chasing around in the mines after unknown enemies and probably did not really know what to be looking for. Zacxh led them away from Sorn's planned route and lost them easily by dashing down a corridor too narrow for them to follow. Actually it was too narrow for Zacxh as well, but he bent his tortured body into unlikely positions and arched his back horribly as he sprinted. He hardly missed a step and left cursing frustrated pursuers behind. They would probably report being eluded by some sort of Priest helping stone daemon. They would not be too far off.

Zacxh circled around and made his way stealthfully to the ambush site. He hoped he was not too late. As he crept through the mines enroute, he heard several bellowed admonishments and commands. The voice was unmistakable. Dinlin had taken the bait.

Zacxh arrived by a back route to the kill zone and found everyone in place. Even Inarra had quit her roost and was lurking behind a stalagmite. Center stage was Valron with his Trident set to receive Dinlin's charge. This was going to be ugly.

Sorn showed up a few tense minutes later. To his credit, he looked confident and pleased. He indicated with a hand signal that all was well and disappeared into a hiding spot. The Spiderguard waited.

The racket Dinlin made as she stormed alone around the corner of the killing floor was a cacophony of clanking plate mail, a mace banging absently across a heavily armored thigh and the nonstop montage of a thousand mumbled curses.

She spotted Valron and paused a moment, as the realization of the treachery afoot bloomed across her in wave of visible heat.

Valron delivered a carefully practiced line designed to incite the priestess into a blind rage.

'Okay bitch... Make my Day!'

It worked perfectly. Dinlin charged. Trynn charged. Zacxh charged. Everyone but Valron charged. He just stood, one foot bracing the base of his razor sharp trident and received her attack.

The effectiveness of his tactic was unbelievable. The priestess was nearly blind in her rage and rammed into the trident with arms raised and flailing. Valron ducked his head under her clumsy mace attack and skidded several paces back under the impact of the mortally wounded priestess who died hanging limp and completely impaled upon the haft of his spear. Two tips of the brutally effective weapon protruded all the way through the Priestess, and out the solid steel back plate of her mail.

Zacxh reacted too slowly to stop his attack and finished his charge with a mighty backhanded swing. The Priestesses head flew high and landed upside down and still blinking at Nal's feet with a meaty thud. Nal looked at it coolly as the creepy thing breathlessly tried to spit out one last curse. The bloodless lips paused forever mid phrase and Nal would long wonder exactly which circle of Loth’s hell his soul had been relegated to.

There was surprisingly little blood, her heart must have been either bisected or mashed into a pulp by Valron's blow, and there was no doubt in anyone's mind that the beheading was completely unnecessary. It was a bit frightening, even for Zacxh, and for a moment everyone looked at Valron in awe.

Valron looked around proudly and spoke with rare humor.

'Well quit gawking and help me get this thing off my spear. We still have a lot of work to do.'

The Spiderguard jumped to action. Down the hall Zacxh thought he heard the sound of bats and of a falling elf. Sorn was grinning evilly.

Revelations.

The Spiderguard lost themselves deep into the mines well away from any likely avenue of pursuit. Inarra posted her crystalline companion down the shaft as a sentry and after Dinlin's body was burnt to greasy ash and dusted off a cliff, the Spiderguard queried Rorlint about the ridiculous accusation that he was a priest.

The answer was shocking. Not only was he a self proclaimed priest, but he proved it by miraculously healing Sorn’s leg and back wounds. He described a small but growing secret sect of anti Loth priests working together and recruiting members to one day overthrow the Spider Queen's hold on the dark elves of the Underdark.

Zacxh could hardly believe his ears. His dreams were coming true in a way which everything he thought he knew told him was not possible. He held his tongue and listened. Rorlint described his cult as followers of the god 'Rauvdril,' the patron deity of Strength, Courage, and Travel. He said the cult held to ten simple rules of action. They were as follows:

1. Courage – be brave, be strong, do not show fear

2. Prowess – be excellent, be admired, be skilled, especially on the battlefield

3. Exploration – find new paths, new places, new things

4. Self-Reliance – don’t expect others to do your work for you

5. Discipline – stay the course, show inner strength, focus on the goal

6. Self-Improvement – always strengthen yourself to strengthen Rauvdril

7. Exert your Will – shape your environment, don’t let it shape you

8. Force is a Last Resort – save your strength for the times it is truly needed

9. Act with Determination – when the time comes to act, be swift and strong

10. Inspire Loyalty – impress and draw others to you by living these virtues

The rules resonated strongly within Zacxh. He knew immediately upon hearing them that he would add them to his budding moral code. He was anxious to meditate on how the rules would bear upon his ideas of Honor and Loyalty, but for now, he simply knew that Rauvdril had one new follower. Zacxh was familiar with the idea that any enemy of Loth was his friend, but this just might be much more that that.

The Spiderguard discussed Rorlint's fate. Zacxh proposed that the 'bait' meaning Rorlint be hidden with supplies somewhere deep and inaccessible in the mines. All agreed that hiding him close was preferable to sending him alone into the Underdark and the death he would certainly find there. Rorlint explained that he could survive a long while without food or provisions sustained merely by his faith which was but further evidence to Zacxh that He had found a like minded spirit in the priest. At any rate, the decision to secret him off was made and it became merely a matter of where and how.

Valron interjected and took Rorlint by the hand. He led the priest to the edge of the precipice, scooped him up like a bride, spoke a prayer to Rauvdril and jumped over. The two plummeted down only to be barely seen swooping across the chasm. They disappeared into the gloomy glow of evening headed to some remote and inaccessible hiding spot. It was unlike any kind of levitation any of them had ever seen. It was clearly something different, something mere nobility did not explain.

Valron was only gone a few minutes, but upon return faced a barrage of inquiry. His answer astounded Spiderguard. He too was a priest of Rauvdril. His tale was long and complicated but explained much of why he was disowned for so long by his house. He was a heretic noble in search of glory and a fighting force for Rauvdril. He saw the Spiderguard as a possible fit for that need and Rorlint as his means of communication to the movement. That is why he was reserved about the planning of the Rescue, he did not want to tip his hand, and that is why he was so happy to dispatch Dinlin. The priests of Rauvdril were of course sworn to war with and eventually overthrow Loth.

Zacxh could see by the way his friend and sergeant looked at him, that Valron knew that Zacxh had already become a neophyte follower of Rauvdril. The two paused a moment looking at each other and a bond unlike any Zacxh knew formed between the two. It was more than friendship, more than unity of purpose. It was a certainty of loyalty beyond words or contract. It was divine.

Zacxh suddenly understood why he had been so fond of Valron all this time, despite all their differences. He understood why he, a slave, had always felt a kinship with his Noble leader. And he suddenly knew with a comforting inner glow what his life was about. He was a knight of Rauvdril albeit a secret one and only he and possibly Valron knew it. The thought made his head spin.

A quick glance about his unit gave Zacxh an amazing revelation. The entire guard was made of elves who had cause to follow Valron on his holy mission. Everyone but Inarra that is, and she was too self absorbed to really object. If only Captain Xulleyl knew what she had wrought in this unit, she would probably kill herself out of shame, or more likely out of the justified fear of punishment by Loth's daughters.. House Oussund was now an incubator of heresy and rebellion. Zacxh loved it. All that talk of slavery and oppression within the guard was now turned to thoughts of freedom and the liberation of all elves. Well, not burn elves. They would have to suffer on as always... the vile bastards.

At any rate, esoteric matters aside it was time to cover their tracks literally. Zacxh Nal and Inarra set out on a patrol which found its way to some mine guards. They were in a frenzy looking for Dinlin and the escaped priest. Nal and Inarra bluffed beautifully and Zacxh kept well inside his mental hiding place to avoid giving anything away. Eventually, the patrol found Captain Xulleyl. She gave them a run down of the escape and told them to keep an eye out for anything unusual. The order was a relief, apparently, they were not yet implicated.

The patrol returned to the secret mine. The Spiderguard returned to business as usual to assuage suspicions among their miners and conscripts, but soon A knock came at the door. It was Xulleyl. She was clearly suspicious of the escape. Who better to recognize the handiwork of her Guard? She asked a few open questions inviting someone to betray or implicate them self, but the Spiderguard knew better than to answer. There were a few tense looks, and in the end it was clear that Xulleyl had no doubt about exactly what had happened, but as planned, she really did not want to know. Her questions told the guard two things. First, it was a silent endorsement of the mission. She was apparently in the Nal camp about the danger of having a loose cannon like Dinlin rolling about Nerr’tenden. Second, it was a warning that the murder could never be discussed openly with her. It was quite obvious that she required deniability in this matter. She told the Spiderguard everything she knew about what was seen and heard and of the ongoing efforts do discover more. This was ostensibly to help them investigate, but everyone knew she was giving them the tools they needed to cover their tracks. Then she gave an order to 'keep your eyes open' and departed.

The Spiderguard jumped to action yet again. Three patrols went out to trample up and obscure tracks. Zacxh Sorn and Inarra had a special mission to obscure the site where they disposed of Dinlin's body. They arrived at the scene of the crime about the same time as Rath.

Rath was apparently a very skilled tracker and was on the brink of uncovering a key piece of evidence, only she did not know it. Zacxh was horrified that he might be too late to avert the disaster when he saw Inarra give one of the most seldom used secret hand signals of the Spiderguard. 'Dazzle them with Bullshit'

Zacxh dove right in. What followed was both terrifying and amazing. Zacxh and Inarra trampled around the scene of the crime laying new prints over old in an indecipherable maze of random tramplings. All the while Zacxh plied the furious tracker with insincere offers to purchase back his Concealment cloak and Inarra tried to flamboyantly broker the impossible deal with flowery words and a pleading simpering manner that finally had poor Rath so distracted and infuriated that she drew steel and nearly attacked.

Zacxh drew his blade and advanced further escalating the confrontation and trampling all over possible clues. Rath was beside herself with anger at the defilement and the confrontation ended with Inarra and Zacxh storming off indignant. Inarra had a small rapier cut on her throat, and Zacxh tossed in the final word as he put up his huge sword and trooped away.

'Well you unreasonable wench, you can just consider my offer officially revoked!'

Sorn stayed behind and offered diplomacy and help. To all outward appearance the Spiderguard were as disjointed and undisciplined as ever, and their tracks were trampled into complete oblivion. Mission complete.

The following flow cycle, Xulleyl returned to the secret mine. She told The Spiderguard of a long range and time consuming mission to investigate tattooed spies in Karsolithuyl and report to Lashara. They were to leave immediately.

She also mentioned the incidental fact that a formal inquisition was enroute to Nerr’tenden and that regrettably they would have to be absent during the inquiry. If there was any piece of evidence they would like forwarded to the Priestesses of the inquisition, particularly about swarms of bats, or invisible creatures running at high rates of speed across the ceiling... anything like that, they should make written affidavits and forward them to her office so that no stone was left unturned. Otherwise, their mission to Karsolithuyl was too important for delay and they were to depart at first glow.

It seemed the Captain knew how to protect her interests. In the eyes of the church, she would be as guilty as the rest if any plot were uncovered. On the other hand, if no evidence were found, long elven tradition held that no further action would ever be visited upon the matter. The inquiry would be a frightening but hopefully fruitless event.

The Spiderguard quickly made preparations to leave the mine. Orders were passed out among the conscripts and the miners to continue the secret dig, and the party departed back to Nerr’tenden.

The Barracks had been ransacked. Sorn asked around a bit and it seemed likely that Ardul was behind it. The only item missing from the equipment was Inarra's large stockpile of poisons. It seemed strange that Ardul wanted all that poison but there was just no reading that elf. Valron rounded up the guard and decided it best to leave immediately.

On the way out Spiderguard stopped by Captain Xulleyl's quarters to report the missing poison. They were trooping down the hall to her door, and passed Zalch’s on the way. It was open and Zacxh heard the end of an exchange between Flick and Xulleyl. Zalch was killed in the Orlylyl attack, and apparently Flick had been promoted to Lieutenant and was taking over Zalch’s unit. It seemed a strange course for Xulleyl. to take, but Zacxh never seemed to know what motivated the captain and just had to trust that the decision was a wise one. Captain Xulleyl spotted her Spiderguard and bid them enter and congratulate the new Lieutenant. Zacxh was the first to speak his congratulations and good wishes. How well he knew the danger and burden of command. Zacxh truly hoped Flick could rise to the position.

Captain Xulleyl received the report of the missing poison and of the ransacked barracks in her office without any show of alarm. She merely nodded, offered another chance to illuminate any information they might have about Dinlin's disappearance, then encouraged them to be under way. She did not have to ask twice. The Spiderguard departed and worked hard to put some quick miles between themselves and Nerr’tenden. There would be some hard feelings ahead when they passed through Illium’s outpost, but better face that than torment and almost certain death at the hands of the inquisitors of Loth.

The Spiderguard fell into its standard traveling formation. Zacxh and either Sorn or Inarra walked point about twenty paces ahead of Valron and Nal. Trynn generally pulled rear guard. The kept moving as quickly as possible without going so fast that they were likely to stumble into an ambush. It would have been nice to pass the long hours of the march chatting, but Zacxh knew enough to keep his mouth shut and his eyes and ears open for danger.

Soon the gates of Bhaerynden were in sight ahead. Zacxh wondered what kind of reception they would receive and as expected he and his companions were immediately recognized. They were broken into three groups of two, and paraded into the outpost of Bhaerynden like prisoners. The guards of Bhaerynden seemed likely to disarm Zacxh and his crew, but a few fawning looks from Inarra and some hard ones from Zacxh changed the tide of that action.

The Spiderguard was brought to a secure room with many guards and forced to audience with Illium. The awkwardly gaited dancer tromped into the room and offered very dubious sounding gratitude for the manner in which his body had been delivered several months earlier. Indeed, Illium's brush with death had left him pale, and somewhat diminished. He was alive certainly, but somehow not so completely as before. Sorn eased tensions by offering to dance with the paranoid noble, but he politely declined and sent The Guard on it's way. All in all the audience went as well as could be expected, but was nonetheless exceedingly tense. During most of the meeting Trynn and Valron and Zacxh spent most of their time staring at their feet and letting Inarra and Sorn charm Illium into a charitable mindset.

The Guard left Bhaerynden as soon as it was polite to do so and again made good time as they strode toward the catacombs.

The Catacombs were quiet, and uneventful. They had a fairly bad reputation amongst caravans and small civilian bands, but Zacxh expected that even had Nal not recently stripped the endless maze of brigands, a heavily armed force of elves like the Spiderguard which was traveling without cargo was in little danger.

Soon The Spiderguard came upon the narrow bridges of Llurth Dreier. They could have bypassed the city entirely, but Sorn felt it would be rude to do so and impressed upon Valron the importance of visiting the prime. Valron acquiesced on condition that the Spiderguard press on at first glow.

As always the prime was elegant and gracious. He had a great table laid with a supper so fine that even Zacxh took a tiny nibble and sip here and there. Sorn and Trynn danced and tumbled as entertainment, but were only marginally impressive. The Prime was understanding of the lackluster performance and chalked it up to fatigue and hunger.

The baths in Llurth Dreier were magnificent and Zacxh excused himself as soon as possible to experience them. He tipped the bath master well and in the morning, when Valron and Sorn came looking for him, the scarred warrior and all his gear were clean oiled and polished to a fresh smelling and glowing luster. They awoke him from a happy doze near the floral pool. Zacxh stood up and stretched like a spent lover and walked away from the baths with obvious remorse. The bath master bid him farewell by name.

'Come again Knife. Our doors are always open...'

Sorn Filled Zacxh in on what he had missed at the feast. The prime opened up to Sorn after a few drinks, and told of old treacheries and intrigues. The short of it was that the prime told a tale of how house Aleanana ordered the destruction of house Rilynrae for reasons of religious intolerance. House Noquiryn carried out the deed, but would not have been successful without Noquiryn backing. Sorn relayed the information dispassionately, but Zacxh was certain that there was tremendous emotional tension just below the surface. It seemed to Zacxh that there were more surprises yet bottled up in young Sorn.

Spiderguard was under way soon after. They passed Dallnothax and Bhaerynden Remembering the recent meeting at the Noquiryn outpost with visiting High Priestess Lashickk.

They made way quickly through Dusklyngh happily aware that every outpost became less complicated and easy to pass the farther it lay from Nerr’tenden. It was shocking to Zacxh to think that only months before he had never dared to venture even so far as to the upstairs level of his smallish keep well behind the Crystal gates and now he tromped about the Underdark on house business as if he owned the place. Not only did he have no fear of being confronted, he relished the thought as a way to hone his weak negotiation skills and strong combat ones.

The Spiderguard moved through the glimmering Sapphire gates of Goliderth quickly. They did not need any extra attention in this Ossund outpost and tried as much as possible to just slip through unnoticed. They wore house Oussund uniforms and were efficient enough in their activities to pass without much fuss. There was plenty of time to learn the outpost better once Xulleyl joined them in several weeks to meet with and report to Lashara.

Spiderguard departed the same morning that they arrived. It was not until they were wee out of sight of the outpost, that Sorn remembered one of the other things that The Prime had told them over dinner. Sorn informed Zacxh that there were Illithid... Mind Flayers about. Numerous caravans were lost and most of the few bodies that had been found had neat holes in their skulls where the brains seemed to have been sucked out.

Zacxh picked up on a strange stillness and feeling of foreboding as they traveled this last part of the journey. Everyone was jumpy and Zacxh caught himself looking over his shoulder at every opportunity. He could not put his finger on why he felt like that. It was odd. What’s more, he was sure that he heard strange noises which were not quite voices but which nevertheless expressed a range of feelings and ideas like pride, comfort, shame, and fear. The sounds were on the very edge of perception and whenever Zacxh strained his ears to hear better, the voices seemed to fade into nothingness.

Trynn and Inarra seemed jumpy too and it did not take Zacxh long to come up with a theory of why.

Illithid.

Every elf in the Underdark had heard the stories. Missing caravans, mysterious disappearances sometimes the wholesale slaughter of entire communities. The stories were cautionary tales about the dangers of lone travel the evils of the unknown and of the most pressing reason for unity among the Dark elves. Illithid were the only threat around for Elves, They moved in small groups and seemed to lack social organization on a large scale so their danger was countered by elven militia, but now and then small raiding parties appeared within elven society and all hell broke lose.

Zacxh had a horrible hunch that here in the passages outside Karsolithuyl, all hell was about to cut loose. He slowed his pace, listened and peered and put all his senses to work. This might be a good chance to see up close what all the fuss about the Illithid was about. They were supposed to be black and slimy like some sort of ugly walking fish. Zacxh heard tales of how they stunned their victims with some sort of mental force. It was aid to feel like being effected by brown mamba poison only the effects were felt on your whole body. Zacxh wondered if he, or rather the violent forces which motivated him in battle, might be immune.

  • Mind Flayers.

The Spiderguard patrolled down the winding naturally formed passage carefully. The sense of foreboding which touched Zacxh and Inarra, weather through association or through there own ill tuned mental sensitivities, soon spread to the entire group. Every elf had their senses hyper tuned and they all snapped their eyes around and looked over their shoulders and odd shadows and phantoms which, under scrutiny, inevitably disappeared.

The first confirmation of danger came in the form of a decimated caravan. A dozen merchants and escorts lay dead upon the ground. All had telltale wounds. Their heads were split and empty and many had the characteristic facial bruising and scarring which the Spiderguard would soon know belied an Illithid attack.

Nal and Valron looked the bodies over while Inarra and Zacxh pressed ahead to watch for ambush. Trynn stayed back and watched the rear. While they were surveying the bodies, Zacxh spotted a funny flaw in a corner of the passageway. There was something suspicious about the way the stone of the ceiling fused with the wall. It looked... artificial.

Zacxh raised his hand giving the Spiderguard signal for 'Freeze, I see enemy,' The Spiderguard instantly fell silent and still. Sorn crept up to Zacxh and gave a pleading look to know what was going on. Zacxh pointed to the aberration of stone and Sorn instantly saw it as well. Silently Sorn crept unnaturally up the sheer wall and poked his head tentatively through the illusory wall. Inarra pulled the little stone with its annoying imbedded personality from her pocket and dropped to the floor. It sprouted eight hairy legs to land lightly, and scampered of after Sorn.

Sorn held his head through the portal and relayed what he saw back to Zacxh and Inarra via hand signal. '...Three enemy... one small...' then he pulled his head back and retreated across the ceiling toward Valron. Whatever was in there had him a bit spooked. Zacxh slowly drew his sword trying not to make noise and instinctively allowed his skin to harden for a fight. Inarra and he moved up to flank either side of the opening in case something came through in pursuit of Sorn. Inarra wore the slightly absent and a bit frustrated look she always had when communicating mentally with her pet rock.

Inarra's rock was imbued with a great deal of mental energy. So much in fact, that it was conscious and could broadcast its thought strongly enough that anyone who was paying attention and knew how to listen could hear it for short distances. Zacxh heard the rock speaking to Inarra.

Inarra was trying to get an idea of what was behind the fake wall, and her pet rock was describing new 'friends.' Zacxh had little doubt that the so called friends were somehow psionically duping the stone and plying it for Intelligence about its owners. Zacxh took a calming breath and allowed some of his mental force to distance his mind from the situation. Normally he would have enhanced his strength by emulating the form and power of an ogre, instead he enhanced his calm and detachment by emulating the aspect of a cobra. He absently flicked his tongue out and mostly tried in vain to taste the air. It tasted stale... if anything. Zacxh lacked the sensory organs of a snake, just its detachment.

Sure enough it was only a moment before a short gooey looking humanoid child walked through the fake wall and right into the ambush. it had a black octopus for a head and slimy dark skin. It radiated almost no heat as if cold blooded or recently dead. It moved in a creepy, limp way with exaggerated and dramatic arm motions. It had large pupiless black eyes wide set and leering. Illithid were shorter that he had imagined.

Zacxh heard... or felt... a little squeal of fear as he lunged into his attack. He plunged his blade deep into the surprised creature's belly. He saw the thing lurch in pain but felt almost no resistance once the tip of his blade penetrated the tough and thick but strangely slippery and yielding skin. The inside of the thing was all jelly and cartilage. Creepy.

Zacxh pulled his blade free with a disgusted grimace on his face. It slid out easily covered in disgusting Ichors. Zacxh spun his blade to try to drive it strait into the beast's heart, but the Illithid retreated a step and looked ready to try to side step his attack. It was a hopeless gambit, this beast of nightmares was overmatched and knew it. Zacxh and the thing stood frozen a moment in the strangeness of the momentum certainty of this confrontation almost made Zacxh feel a bit sorry for his foe.

Suddenly, Zacxh realized that the creature was not sentimentally pondering it's death as Zacxh was, it was launching a powerful mental attack. Zacxh felt as though hit by a shockwave of mental energy. The energy of the attack belied the slight size of its origin and though the energy was 'deflected' by the sturdy walls of Zacxh's mental fortress, he was shaken from his reverie and knew he had better dispatch the thing before it lashed out again.

Zacxh moved to slowly, and missed his chance to finish the fight. The three tips of Valron’s trident erupted from the Illithid’s sternum and strange smelling sticky goop splashed Zacxh’s chest and arms. Just like the lively slithering arms of Dinlin's snake headed whip had, the wriggling tentacles dancing afore the creatures face fell limp as it died impaled upon the end of Valron’s mighty weapon.

Zacxh looked up into Valron's arms to give him a nod of approval just as two more numbing waves of buzzing tingling energy slammed him from the rear. The first one was deflected by the walls of Zacxh's mind, but the second blasted through like a freezing gale through a broken window. These waves were an order of magnitude stronger than those of the small form on Valron's trident. Zacxh realized the small one was just a child. That struck Zacxh as strange as he had heard somewhere that Illithids were... created in some mysterious ritual fully grown, but he had little time to ponder.

With his mental fortress breached, Zacxh had to fight off the daze left in the wake of the assault by pure force of will. He spun to charge the much more dangerous adults and forced down waves of vertigo and nausea. He felt tingly and numb all over like an arm feels when it has been rested upon improperly. He could not feel his extremities and had to confirm visually that he still held his sword in numb hands as he attacked.

All around, Zacxh saw Spiderguard members slump and drop weapons from deadened hands as wave after wave of stunning mental power flowed over the group. The combat was ugly. Despite his efforts to maintain his mental walls, maybe half the stunning blasts broke through and twice Zacxh dropped his sword from unresponsive hands. It was intensely unpleasant. The Illithid adults died from the combined and clumsy blows of the entire guard. Zacxh was left feeling empty and abused in a way that no physical attack had ever made him feel. It was abundantly clear why his race was hated these vile creatures. Zacxh looked at the black giggly corpses. The monsters seemed more like sea creatures that humanoids. It was as if some squid had simply perched upon a internally decomposed and headless corpse.

The Spiderguard entered the den of the Illithids and found the remains of a ten member caravan. Six yet lived, the others had been... harvested.

They were tied and apparently just waiting their turn to be made into a meal. The remaining leader of the group, probably second in command before their abduction, spoke fir the group. They were a Mythral caravan. Valron decided to free them with their cargo and send them on their way. Zacxh, exhausted from maintaining his mental defenses, barely registered the exchange.

The Spiderguard departed and traveled quickly in an effort to reach the outpost before they were set upon again by tentacled foes. They did not make it.

The second battle transpires much as the first. Inarra found a false wall, and Zacxh Valron and Trynn charged in to try to dispatch the beasts by surprise before the could mount their psychic attack.

The tactic failed. This chamber contained four Illithid. Zacxh's mental barriers again protected him about half the time and he shrugged off a few blasts, but he still found himself groping several times for his dropped sword. Trynn and Valron fares worse and Zacxh only peripherally noticed in his dazed state that sometime during the fight, Falt the skilled and frightful Loth's Fang disciple fled the battle. They must have stumbled across him in a fight with the 'Mind Flayers.' Zacxh found his departure from the midst of the fight especially rude in light of the fact that he did not so much as say thank you for his rescue.

The battle lasted for less than a minute, but during that time Zacxh expended more mental effort than he had collectively during the previous month. When the battle ended and the last Illithid dropped, so did Zacxh, uninjured but exhausted in a way that no physical effort could bring. His head pounded painfully with the beating of his heart and his hands shook from rage and fear and adrenalin. The daemons of Zacxh's mind were entirely expended. He had always speculated that such a completely drained mental state as this might be pleasant... a break from his internal horror. It wasn't. Zacxh felt vulnerable and alone. He knew that without hate and anger do drive his body past his physical limits; he would be just as vulnerable in a fight as any 'unskilled' warior. Sure he could swing and parry and kill, maybe better than any in the guard. But still he felt naked and vulnerable. The walls were no longer avenues of attack and escape but just... walls. His skin was no longer armor... just skin, and if he were hurt or cut open by the random lucky attack, he would limp and weaken and eventually fall.

Zacxh remembered how he used to so often fight in the pit in this weakened and vulnerable state. It was terrifying and every attack could have meant his doom. Indeed he had been laid low many times. He clung to the memory and 'awakened' it in his mind. It flared into being within his mind and gave him strength, a flickering ember where once an inferno of power had blazed in the hearth of his soul. Zacxh stood and stretched his tingling and numb limbs. His mind and body awoke to their surroundings. The Spiderguard was busy searching the monsters and their slimy lair. Trynn ran off in pursuit of Falt and Valron commanded that the guard move out.

Zacxh danced from foot to foot to restore feeling in his legs and gathered up his dropped weapons and a few bits of coin which lay strewn about. He flexed the hard hands within his armored gauntlets and readied himself for the ugly fights ahead. He needed sleep desperately and longed for the first time in weeks for something to eat. As far as he was concerned, the sooner they reached Karsolithuyl, the better.

It was not far now...
