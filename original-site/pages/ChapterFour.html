Inarra leaned heavily against the wall after her mental assault on the enemy captain, skin flushed and one cheek smudged prettily. She watched his face contort, and then he fell as if in slow motion, Xulleyl stepping back out of his way. Inarra's eyes snapped to the seriously injured Xulleyl, who moved to the nearest wall. Xulleyl's skin and equipment were smoldering and cracked from the fireball's detonations, her lungs heaved wetly with the ragged holes the sword had made on its passage through her body. Two more cuts bled heavily from her side and weapon arm.

Xulleyl let the wall hold up her weight while she retrieved a small leathery packet from a tiny belt pouch. She opened the top and poured a fine powder into her mouth, then swallowed. Her wounds didn't appear any better, but she seemed to gain some strength from the dose. She carefully closed the packet and replaced it in the tiny belt pouch. She moved back to the fallen enemy and cleaned her blade on his cloak, then sheathed it in a single fluid motion.

Standing erect, she quickly walked back down the passage, snapping an order back over her shoulder: "Bring him." She walked on without looking back.

Zacxh had the dead captain draped over his shoulder as he walked down the hallway. He was visibly injured though his face betrayed no trace of pain, as always it was expressionless and bland.

His leg was torn open by a wicked sword slash, and he bled from three or four other nasty cuts. Three crossbow bolts protruded from various locations; one snapped off at the shaft, presumably to allow him the movement of his right arm. His new fine cloths were torn in several locations by sword cuts that did not seem to leave any mark on the underlying flesh and every scar on his body stood up angrily. He trailed an odor of ozone and burnt hair and small wisps of smoke traced the passage of both he and his charred cargo as he limped down the corridor.

The Spidergaurd rallied about their severely injured Captain and in the several inevitable moments of rushed orders and consolidation, Zacxh found himself standing near Kadyel.

He looked at her as if to speak actually quirking his eyebrow a moment, then decided better. Without comment he hefted the dead captain onto his smoldering shoulder and moved off toward the 'Headquarters'. The dripping trail of blood he left behind would not be difficult to follow.

Clearly burned and torn up, but nonetheless the proud glint remaining in her eye, Kadyel smiles at Zacxh as she catches up to him. Once the dark shadow of the healing magic fades into Zacxh's wounds, she moves to grabs the dead captain's legs and together carry the body.
