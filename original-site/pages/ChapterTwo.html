ChapterTwo

By twos and threes, the remaining five members of 'The Barracks' arrived. They were: Sorn a handsome male dancer lightly armed, Inarra A gorgeous and aloof debutant with a distain for all the lesser 'Little People' in the room, Kadyel A huge tough looking female warrior, (Gill) A lightly armored and quiet male, and Trynn a shifty unarmed male slave. Introductions were made, and it was immediately apparent that the there were three classes of elf in the room. The slaves, the privileged, and the aloof. Normally, Zacxh would have gravitated to Trynn as slaves will often do, but there was something shifty and sinister about the tough looking brawler which turned Zacxh away. Sorn the dancer seemed the friendliest of the lot but plied his attentions primarily upon Innara. (Gil) was quiet and watchful and Kadyel held slaves in obvious distain and so seemed to Zacxh a typical female hard case. Zacxh surveyed the room one last time and decided that his choice of Valron was his best bet of not being killed as he rested.

Hours passed, and people had felt each other out a bit when food arrived by armed guard. The atmosphere felt more like a prison than a barracks.

Zacxh of course waited in a corner until all the diners had eaten, then he picked over a few scraps left on various plates. The food was fantastic. he hadn't eaten so well in years. He downed a leftover cup of water and stacked the platters and cups neatly for retrieval.

The Lieutenant arrive awhile thereafter. He instructed that the troupe was to arm itself and follow to a meeting with the Captain. The party straggled out onto the hallways and were led a twisting route to a large natural cavern adorned in stalagmites with a high ceiling. The group moved in and spread out a bit. Zacxh moved away from the others a bit, sensing that they might be in a trap of some sort. When the Captain came in, that fear was confirmed. She looked to be a seasoned and tough warrior. She told the group that their identities were no longer and that she was setting up a task force of skilled warriors and adventurers. There were to be many tests ahead. She had bought the freedom of the slaves and that...

Zacxh did not hear the rest. He struggled with the meaning of her words, awed by the possible prospect of freedom. He looked at the others and saw that they were glancing around with a heightened urgency. What had she said? Something about finding a leader? Something about ...the strongest surviving and trying not to actually kill one another...

"Oh crap!" Zacxh was in a pit with all his new companions and they were meant to fight it out to see who was to lead.

Zacxh instantly dropped to a crouch and bolted to a dark corner. Thankfully he was wearing the scouting cloak and he whispered its activation word. He crouched behind a boulder, and the cloak helped him to blend in. Then he waited. Despite his concealment, Inarra pointed him out to her temporary allies. She had some sort of uncanny vision. Zacxh retreated deeper into the cave out of her view. Fortunately she was the first to fall.

There was a moment of confusion as people attacked their neighbors and some incantations brought powerful blasts of various forms of energy which brought several to their knees. Zacxh waited only a moment before he charged (gil) who seemed unaware of his approach.

Zacxh needed allies and negotiated aggressively. He slashed his blade across the nape of (gil)'s neck opening a not quite debilitating wound. Zacxh quickly offered him the option of 'join me or fall right here by my blade'. (gil) pause only a moment. Unfairly wounded as he was he was clearly outmatched and nodded his assent. Zacxh took a moment to enter his combat trance. The arena seemed to drop away from his mind and he attained his painless tireless detached state. (gil) crept around a stalagmite and fired his crossbow. Zacxh move the other way and crept up behind Valron in a flanking maneuver. Gil engaged the mighty warrior frontally and feeling a pang of conscience, Zacxh impaled the powerful warrior from behind. In his trance he saw the trident wielding warrior's energies flowing about his body. Zacxh thrust his blade into the nexus of thos energies and delivered a wound that would have killed anyone else in the room outright but which merely disabled Valron. While wrenching his blade free of the proud warrior and turning to find a new hiding spot, Zacxh heard his friend hit the floor with a pained grunt.

Zacxh found a new stoop and watched in awe as the party hacked and blaster each other down. He assisted as (gil) felled Trynn who though unarmed put up a fierce fight. After a few riotous moments it was calm again. Guards dragged the disabled out of the arena and administered enough healing magics to make them conscious. Too much had happened to follow the battle, and Zacxh had been too busy hiding to see who brought down who, so he counted the combatants. Five were out. Three remained. (Gill) Zacxh and... who. He appraised the group again. Sorn, Valron, Trynn, Inarra, Nal... that left...

"Oh great." Zacxh knew his opponent. one of the most powerful warriors of the group. It was Kadyel

Zacxh had spent the last eighty years in situations just like this. His survival hinged on steath cunning and an overwhelming first strike. Zach's consciousness suddenly slammed home into his body again. He could only hold his trance for a short time, and his head ached slightly from the exertion of holding it for too long.

He held his breath and started picking his way slowly around the cavern. The scout was working his way around the other side in plain view. Zacxh had high hopes that (gil) would draw out the remaining combatant and set him up to be flanked. He forced himself into a state of calm and began to recede yet again into his warrior’s trance... and tripped.

"ooof! Guano!" He fell right at the feet of a guard who shook his head at the sad display. It was then that he saw her. She lay prone and unmoving near the base of a stalagmite. He tried his luck.

"Hey Lieutenant, there is another body maybe you should have it healed and moved to watch?"

The guard ignored him. He would have to do it the hard way

"Twang!" (gil)'s crossbow bolt pelted the prone figure. It did not move.

Maybe she really was out of the contest. Perhaps she was dead. Well he would watch and see. (gil) approached Kadyel and was about to give her a kick when she sprung up and stabbed him in the neck with an incredible and horrible thrust of her short sword. He went down hard.

Zacxh focused a moment to built up his mental barriers to pain and shock and focused his now bizarrely acute senses on the movements of the warriors blade. Then he charged.

Kadyel deftly knocked away several clumsy attacks and sent a few of her own. Then with a brilliant spinning lunge ran Zacxh through the liver with her blade. He should have fallen. His body tried to give up and sag, but Zacxh knew too much of pain and the true signs of collapse. He allowed himself a small stagger then jerked his body back off the cruel blade and returned a swipe which lay open Kadyel's arm with a painful yet superficial wound. The huge female circled and lunged several more times. Zacxh narrowly avoided several more blows than saw a momentary lapse in defense. He lunged... and missed, his blow was narrowly deflected by some mystical force.

Kadyel sneered at the slave. "Do you think you can win? Do you really want command?" Her eyes spoke of compromise yet her tone belied treachery and perhaps cruelty. Zacxh considered only a moment. He imagined a continued life of servitude to a cruel and powerful female.

"No, I just don't want *you* to have it!" The two combattents circled each other again. Zacxh had been here so many times before, this was a rare and unbiased fight. He saw another opening, feinted left spun right, and with a stroke of luck drove his blade into Kadyel's right lung.

Fury, pain and disbelief mixed on the face of the warrior as she took a knee, coughed up a bit of blood and toppled over.

The dark wand of the lieutenant quickly stabilized her wound and she shuffled back to the growing spectator area.

Zacxh counted them and verified he was alone in the pit... another pointless victory for the seasoned pit fighter, he lowered his gaze and walked to the Lieutenant to receive his punishment. To his amazement, none came.

As he was congratulated by the Captain, he risked a glance at his... barracks mates. Some regarded him spitefully some with an approving grin and some neutrally. The time for punishment would come soon enough.

The Captain presented him with a spider pin like those of the guards. She also granted him alone among the unit permission to address her. She appointed him leader of the group, yet gave him no avenue of authority. In short, she handed him his cruel fate. He was to lead a unit, if only until his death which would doubtless come quickly.

Zacxh once again cursed the irony of his life. From guard commander to slave to guard commander, an ill fated one once again. As always he felt his future was doomed to pain and failure. He would be a slave or worse soon enough he was sure, besides no decree from a detached Captain would empower him in any way to lead this disaffected band.

Zacxh placed the Spiderguard pin in his pouch and followed the guards back to the barracks. He fell into a deep exhausted trance immediately upon setting his head upon the pillow.

"Valron, do us both a favor and kill me now if you must. The suspense is murder"

"Nah, too tired. Maybe tomorrow..." came the handsome warriors reply as Zacxh passed out.

Each of the battle weary band lowered themself to rest that night in more pain than they had experienced in a long time. The bed (or the floor, in Trynn’s case) is heavenly. Whatever it has been, ever since they left Dolthgurdar, your life has been a series of unexpected turns. Today was no different.

Sleep is uninterrupted, and in the morning, all feel quite a bit better (non-lethal damage gone, lethal damage healed 8 hours). Shortly after they wake up, the door is unlocked and two guards come in with platters of food. They clear the old plates and pitchers. Someeone must have been made happy – today’s breakfast is delicious, and even includes some fish. As the guards prepare to leave, one of them goes over to Zacxh and surprises all by making a short bow.

“Sir, Lt. Ardul commands that your troops be ready for a trip to the armory within an hour.” With that, he leaves a surprised but unaffected looking Zacxh and exits the common room, locking it behind him.

Trynn of course is waiting for others to eat first. He specifically sequesters some portion of the food before Valron can sprinkle it with whatever he's got in those damn bottles.

After observing for a few minutes, he joins in the meal. You see him nervously fidgeting, obviously working up the gumption to speak. After a few moments where no one else speaks, he offers this.

"Now that we've played Xulleyl's game and beaten each other down it would serve us well to think this through. We are weakened, and some of us may be cowed or at least appear so... ". (Trynn glances at Inarra's bruised jaw)

"But we have no idea if Xulleyl is what she says she is. We're out here on the edge of nowhere, and she had us disappeared from society. She could have anything in store for us. She could be a traitor, using us as tools in her traitorous schemes against House Oussund. By Lolth's Bloated Belly! She could be from a different House entirely, setting us up as part of some twisted plot. All the while us thinking we serve Oussund with her. How would we know? I can tell you this much. She murdere one of Oussund's Lolth priestesses as part of arranging for my fake death. I'm yet to be convinced she is loyal to Oussund."

Trynn nods toward Sorn. "She has wasted no time issuing death threats over trivialities. And she keeps us locked in. We're no more than tools to her. She said we must prove ourselves. Well, as far as I'm concerned she must prove herself to us as well."

"But.... if she does prove to be worthy of respect, and if she truly serves House Oussund, I will be willing to commit loyalty. What say you all? Where do you stand? I feel we must be united in this. It is our only hope for success. Can we commit to her, as a group, assuming we determine her intent to be true?"

Trynn looks to each of you, nervously eyeing your reactions. He seems sincere but guarded, which is how he always seems.

Zacxh stands silently in the corner waiting for the others to eat as a good slave should. Suddenly his blank stare disappears to be replace with a grin. He strides over and snatches the biggest and most delicious bit of fish from the platter just as Inarra is reaching for it. He stuffs the oversized bit in his mouth, and with a bewildered look on his face realizes this it the best food he has ever eaten. Then he begins to speak

"Arouft, lefght gre..."

"As for the `contest', I think we all learned a lot out there, especially that this is no group to be trifled with. I attribute my victory more to fate than to martial skill, and I have no great faith in the benevolence of my fate." Zacxh pauses a moment to scratch the torn and tattered remains of his right ear.

"The captain asked what we learned out there. Here is what I saw. Divided, strong though we may be, we are already defeated. So. Here is my second proposal. I propose a creed of warriors. I propose that we commit to one another here and now. We will not survive our training if we do not pull together. Individuals have no place in a group like this. I have proposed to some of you that we watch each others backs. Now I propose it to you all. Furthermore, though not a leader, I will act as watchman, and any elf who acts in a harmful way toward anyone in this room will answer to my blade."

"I expect one of two outcomes from this. Either I will be dead by the morrow, or we will become one of the great units of the Underdark. I have already been handed threats due to my unenviable position as `leader.' Well so be it. I have very little to lose in death, and if we do not pull together death is what we shall have."

Zacxh picks up the pin, stuffs it unceremoniously into his pouch and grabs a loaf of sweat bread which he stuffs into his mouth with a swig of water to chase it down.

"By the way in answer to Trynn's question, I was captured, enslaved and beaten by house Oosund. The priestesses of the house conspired and executed my demise. I hold no loyalty to anyone and claim no house. Therefore when I swear my blood to this our "Spidergaurd" there can and will be no conflict of interest."

"I believe we are all `dead'. Perhaps others of you might look at this opportunity as I have?"

Zacxh returns to his corner and resumes his servants posture. Only now, his hand rests lightly on the pommel of his well oiled and polished rapier. His eyes again appear dead.

Valron listens intently to Zacxh's speech and says to him, "Thank you for your words, they are much appreciated." He turns to address the group. "With our acquiescence to yesterday's competition, we in effect, bound ourselves to obey the rules of the Spiderguard. So I would agree that Zacxh should retain his position for this reason, regardless of how we in fact view him amongst ourselves."

In the battle we faced yesterday, you clearly proved yourself worthy in strength, bravery, and cunning. And I find the words that you have just spoken both wise and honorable. Therefore, I for one am willing to place my trust in your leadership. Far more so than I would in any...Spiderguard official I have met thus far."

However, like our leader Zacxh, I commit myself to the protection of this group. And I encourage all of you to pledge the same oath."

Zacxh eyes the floorboards with his usual deferential look and clearly blushes (an ugly medley of pink black and numerous unlikely tones of scar white)

"You are too kind and overstate the case Valron, But I appreciate your commitment to our group."

"I am not worthy to command this group. As yet I am not sure any of us are. Let me for now act as go- fer and whipping boy to the guards and warden of dangers within and without. In that capacity, I hope to help unite our band until we can find our true leader.
