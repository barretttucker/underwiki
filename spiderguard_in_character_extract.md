# SpiderGuard In-Character Extract

This is a cleaned, chronologically ordered extraction of the in-character material and scene-description prose from the uploaded campaign emails.  
I removed most email headers, obvious OOC/meta chatter, and mechanical notes, while preserving the original ordering inside each thread as much as possible.

I also kept a few retrospective narrative summaries when they read like campaign log material, since they help bridge otherwise fragmented exchanges.

## November 2006 — Holding the Mines

Zacxh snorts once then slowly stands up and addresses the group in a way he has not for quite some time.

"Well Valron, I think it is time for you to earn some of that Sergeant's pay. I have been very reluctant to voice my strategic insights, but I will do so now.

"Obviously we have totally lost control of this mining operation. I believe it is a matter of our very survival that we regain it immediately. We can either talk to the Captain or we can simply begin killing off anyone we find down here who is not explicitly part of either Nal's guard or my own handpicked mining detachment. Perhaps we can leave orders with the guardpost to only allow entry by our own handpicked crew. I leave it to you to command our course, but it is suicide to stand and wait for Flick, the Inquisition, or your Aunt to hatch whatever plans they have in motion against us. We need to get proactive and we need to do it now.

"I await your orders, Sergeant. Perhaps you would like for me to go fetch the Captain for a pow wow. Or perhaps you would prefer to dispatch me as a 'messenger' to the unwelcomed Lieutenant.

"This is a time for action. We need to lock down this project right away.

"As for that Inquisitor? He is nothing more than another unwelcomed spy as far as I'm concerned. Captain Xulleyl needs to clear us some space to operate or we need to create it for ourselves. We are already in hot water with the priestesses. One more dead clerical agent matters little.

"Think on this. Perhaps we should collect the mineral, grab the English patient, and get down to our real goal, which is to overthrow Lolth's stranglehold on our people. Perhaps Rauvdryll and his servants can put this mineral to better use than can Captain Xulleyl or whatever vile agent comes up with it at the end of the day.

"Let's use Rauvdryll's power to dig up this mysterious crap and steal it away from the underdark until we can come up with a way to use it to all our advantage.

"It's time to think outside the box, Sergeant, but do it quickly. We need a plan and we need one right now."

Zacxh bends to the floor and bows a warrior's submissive bow to his priest.

"Command me, Valron!"

Myyrix interjects into the conversation.

"Zacxh has it right that we must act before circumstance forces us into a corner, and it seems certain that this Flick and the Inquisitor will have us there in short order unless we take things into our own hands.

"But we might wish to refrain from further killing in the short term. We know that the informant looked to aid us before we drove him off. Perhaps the olive branch might work better than the blade in this case.

"Regardless of what we do, we need to leave the places prepared for us by those that may or may not wish us well, and find a shelter of our own — perhaps somewhere in the mines, surrounded by those selected by you before.

"We know that dangerous forces work against us; we need not make their task any easier by presenting a standing target in a place of their choosing.

"Our goal is in the mines; I say go to the mines."

Nal solemnly speaks up.

"Whatever we choose, if we leave here I can stay in contact with Sharb for reports on the mining progress and situation.

"If Xulleyl does not intend to turn us over, I don't believe we need to flee preemptively. Flick may have assumed the role of our enemy, but she still seems loyal to the Captain. Likewise the inquisitor would not have left willingly were she not willing to accede to the will of the Captain. We may shed what remains of our ability to claim legitimacy if we so quickly vacate this post without more information.

"With the blessing of the Captain, we could 'reassign' the troops that have taken up duty here who are not loyal to us, and prepare for any ill-advised assault on terrain with which we are familiar. If we completely lose this position, we may not be able to reach the target first. If Xulleyl has forsaken us, we will have nothing to gain by staying here.

"We can send one of our elves to inquire as to her decision and eavesdrop on the conversation either with Jayne or through my joined scrying spells. Should we not?"

Trynn regains focus, if only for a moment.

"We should not abandon this position, we should strengthen our hold on it. So I agree with Zacxh. Let us immediately move to clear these mines of any who are not loyal to us. If we must kill some elves to accomplish that, so be it.

"Perhaps we can first try diplomacy. We can relieve Flick as if it is our authority to do so, and technically it is. We can even thank her for holding down the situation while we were gone, and now it is time for her to serve Xulleyl back in Nerr'tenden. If she resists, then we begin killing the elves loyal to her, and perhaps even her if we have to...."

Trynn muses quietly to himself.

"I *would* like to try that move on her again. I think it would work this time..."

"Valron? Surely you have something to say?"

## November 2006 — Flikk, Xulleyl, and the Spy in the Mines

Flikk delivered several swift boots to the head, and Nal fell unconscious. Flikk managed to get her arms in front of her, still manacled, and she pulled Nal up with the manacle chain around his throat as a garrotte just as the party entered. When Myrrix advanced, she threatened to kill Nal with his own manacles unless Xulleyl was brought.

While Trynn was off fetching the captain, Inarra succeeded in brainlocking Flikk. She then went over, removed Nal from the monk's grasp, and waited for the Captain to arrive, offering to make "girl talk" with the immobile Flikk.

Valron cast a Zone of Truth and, when Xulleyl arrived, the party started asking some questions about Flikk while Flikk was still present. The Captain fell prey to the Zone's enchantment and was unable to speak some of her intended answers regarding Flikk. Someone eventually realized that perhaps the Captain should not be asked about Flikk in front of Flikk. Flikk was released from the brainlock on Xulleyl's orders.

Flikk also stumbled trying to answer a question, and after that refused to answer any question directly. Valron later seized on the fact that Flikk refused to answer "Orlylyl" despite being very open with it previously.

"It must be a lie! Orlylyl is being framed."

The Captain confirmed that she considered Flikk a dangerous threat and preferred to keep her close where she could be observed. Xulleyl told the party to lie low in the mines for a while, and agreed to order all of the non-Sharb troops and miners out of the mines so that the party could have control there.

Fresh from the success of the Zone of Truth, the party interviewed all of Sharb's troops and the dozen miners handpicked earlier by Zaxch. Using the Zone of Truth and Inarra's read thoughts ability, they quickly screened all but one of the interviewees as innocent, as far as any dark elf is "innocent."

A spy was uncovered — a moustachioed dark elf named Nekl. When he triggered suspicion, he was quickly passed off to interview room two with Zaxch and Nal. It turned out his real name was Lenk, one of the four Nerr'tenden red dots in the Aleanana map room. He had one of the Eternal Night tattoos.

Meanwhile, Sorn elected to spend two or three days masquerading as the Inquisitor, calling interviews with various people around the outpost, making them wait around and then dismissing them without ever speaking to them. This put more time between the actual murder of the Inquisitor and the exit from Nerr'tenden.

The clever solution was to kill the aide, Sird, while he was still in the Nerr'tenden area, and then have Inarra, disguised as Sird, accompany Sorn, disguised as the Inquisitor, as they exited Nerr'tenden. They would be last seen entering the Catacombs by third parties, while the SpiderGuard would all have alibis back in Nerr'tenden.

Sird was brought by "the Inquisitor" to question the party once more in their mining headquarters. Once inside, all non-SpiderGuard personnel were ordered to leave the room, and then Sorn revealed his disguise. Sird surrendered to an intimidating Myrrix mere instants before Zaxch's whistling sword blade would have cut him in twain. Sird was questioned and found to be doing anything he could to prove that the SpiderGuard, and Flikk incidentally, were guilty.

Inarra burned him with a ray as he sat bound.

The manacles came off clean afterwards.

## December 2006 — The Dig

After what seems an eternity, Zacxh takes a sharp breath and starts awake. He lurches to his feet and in a half-awake panic draws his dagger to fend off an imagined foe. In a moment he comes to understanding and smiles at the likely alarmed SpiderGuard while absently sheathing his blade.

"So, Sergeant, are we ready to get cracking?"

Zacxh looks at the roughly hacked unfinished tunnel before him, then has a thought.

"I know Inarra is leading our crew on a wild goose chase, but maybe this does not have to be so complicated..."

He backs perhaps ten paces from the end and faces the wall to his right. Placing his hand upon the wall, he faces Valron with a quirky smile.

"Better stand back, sir. Those cave-ins were not as hard as you might imagine to induce, but after I pass perhaps Rauvdryll can disguise our passage here."

Zacxh lowers his head and, even as the last vestige of his elven form falls away, begins to force his plow-like head into the roughly hewn wall. For ten yards or so he pushes at a right angle to the charted direction, then turns a winding course directly toward the location indicated by the map, parallel to the planned tunnel to be dug by the mining crews. Rock and dust fly to the heavy percussive stomping of Zacxh's charging, elephant-like feet.

The sounds of tearing stone and earth begin to fade as Zacxh plows on.

He comes back twice to check the direction he's heading with the map's general direction. After a few minutes, something changes and he does not need to check the map any longer: the rock is getting warmer.

He murmurs to himself as he follows the trail.

"Warmer... no, colder... colder. Ah. Warm."

The direction of the tunnel changes slightly, and he continues on until he suddenly stops short. He comes back and takes his normal form. His muscular arm lurches past his own head before he corrects and brings his hand back to lazily scratch at his temple.

"Well, there are voices. I think the rock is talking to me."

At first he thought it was just the whisper of his body against the rocks, but then he kept hearing the sound even when he paused to check the temperature of the stone. When he kept going, it started to sound more like words being whispered to him, or near him, coming from all around with no particular direction.

With that, he grins and transforms into the beast once again, and quickly re-enters the tunnel.

Zacxh digs a while longer until there is a sudden sonic burst followed by a bizarre whimper. A wave of steam washes back through the passage with a distinctly metallic tang.

The party moves through the tunnel in an efficient guard formation, led by Valron, Jayne scouting ahead. The walls and floor are definitely getting warmer. When they brush the rock, they hear the swishing susurrations Zacxh had mentioned — audible without seeming to be heard by the ears, almost as though the sounds are coming from the stone directly into the mind through the skin.

Another minute and the whispers are clearly spoken words, or maybe a chant.

The tunnel curves, and a slight glow appears ahead, throwing a sickly green cast over everyone's vision. The passage opens abruptly into a cavern. Zaxch is off to the side, facing into a wide and tall chamber of alien glowing green stone.

He glances back and starts on seeing them, shifting back to his elven form.

"Hey, you really snuck up on me there! Good job, Valron — I didn't know you could be so quiet! It was really tough to push through that last bit. It was almost as if the rock was pushing back at me."

He is breathing heavily and glistening with sweat.

"I admit I let out a bit of a whimper when I pierced it. The wall screamed at me, and in this form I was over-sensitive. My ears really hurt."

The voices are nearly at conversational volume when the group finally steps through the breach and down into the green chamber. The air is moist, humid, and warm. The chamber is kidney-shaped, open, and strangely smooth, with a circular platform rising from its center and a darker green band of stone high around the wall.

This place is clearly a place of power.

As they glance around at the stone walls, they occasionally catch rainbow reflections. Sometimes they see faces. Sometimes only the green hue as steam rises from the smooth surface.

Sorn requires a moment to recover from the powerful waves of magic that overwhelm his senses. Once he is able, he appraises the strange cavern, his gaze taking in every inch of it, a look of quiet awe developing on his face.

"Sergeant. If we are to explore this place, we should conceal or fill the passage Zacxh made, so that our attentions may be focused on our work.

"And after we do that, I'd like to get a look at the top of that platform up there."

Zacxh looks to Valron and sees a little nod at Sorn's suggestion of filling in the cavern.

"Hmm. I could use a rest anyhow. I'll go block the passage awhile until perhaps one of our casters can better seal it. If any of you wish to leave before I do so, get out now!"

Zacxh waits a heartbeat or two for any who want out, then turns down the rough passage. If any follow, they observe him widening the passage near the hand-dug portion of the tunnel and using couch-sized chunks of granite from his excavation to choke up the narrow bore with the efficiency of a bulldozer in a patch a dozen yards thick.

Then he backs up a dozen yards or so and begins bashing the walls with his rock-hewing head. His beady little eyes gleam with something near joy as he spins and bucks like a raging bull until a small cave-in occurs. The process takes five or ten minutes and, when Zacxh's huge dozer body emerges from the dust and rubble of the stove-in shaft, he transforms to elven form, shakes out his dusty hair, wipes a bit of blood from a minor scratch over his nose, and grins.

"Heh... that cave-in hurt a bit... Your tunnel is secure, Sergeant, though I still recommend we better plug the hole like you did in the map room as soon as it pleases our lord Rauvdryll. For now, I require a bit of rest."

Zacxh pats the dust from his arms as best he can with a slightly disgusted sneer, then settles into a seated position in a quiet corner and closes his eyes.

Valron watches Zacxh go and then speaks to Sorn, his face anxious but intent.

"You better stay here for now, Sorn. I don't want us to touch any more of the stone than we have to. Particularly that platform. I'll go up to observe and then report back to the group."

Valron lifts off the ground and slowly flies over to the platform.

Sorn watches Valron lift effortlessly into the air, wishing that he could do the same. Alas, he cannot.

Sorn turns to Trynn standing nearby and asks quietly, "It's his faith in Rauvdril that grants Valron his powers?"

"Inarra, I think you're the best equipped to monitor the situation beyond the cave-in until Valron is ready to seal the passage more completely."

"Indeed, I will likely need to deal with those inquiring minds beyond. I'll send you all a message directly should I need any of you. Feel free to seal the rubble behind me. Jayne, to me! Until then... behave as you wilt."

Inarra waves a lazy hand and strides purposefully, if a bit sensually, toward the cave-in and disappears rather abruptly with a faint popping sound.

## January 2007 — First Exploration

The rest of the group watches as Valron lifts off to investigate the platform above. He looks around briefly, then murmurs a quick prayer and immediately shades his eyes from the room and the platform. Squinting, he descends.

"Well, there are two flasks up there. Both are very large. One is still stoppered and full of silver liquid; the other is broken, its contents burned their way across the platform's top."

He casts his gaze to the floor, still squinting as though staring at the light of a candle. He points to a spot. The floor's surface is marred and etched in a puddle shape some four feet across.

A quick examination of the other two chambers reveals little. While each room has a similar platform in its center, there are no flasks or etched areas.

One thing of note: the three rooms are absolutely silent except when you are very near to Zaxch's bored entrance hole, where you can faintly hear the voices of the earth. There is no dust or sign of disuse here besides the presence of the single broken flask.

Zacxh opens his eyes slowly and takes three slow and deliberate breaths. He looks toward Sorn and twists his head this way and that until he is rewarded with a soft pop.

"Actually, unless you have some new tricks up your sleeve, we have eight hours or so to waste. As much fun as it was just now to rend the earth, I think I'd better rest a bit before trying it again. Maybe we should try to figure out what this place is all about before we depart. I'm sure Inarra can hold off the inquisitive minds back there until you all can come up with a theory. Until then, and with your permission, Sergeant, I think I may rest awhile."

Zacxh closes his eyes again and seems to fall very quickly back into a restful repose.

Myyrix frowns as he looks over Sorn's shoulder and mutters quietly, "Familiar? Safe?"

After shaking his head, he resumes for all to hear.

"I agree that time is most likely short, and that the other caves bear rapid investigation, but perhaps we should make sure we haven't missed anything and examine the silvered liquid before moving on.

"Lacking a piece of the puzzle for a moment's haste would serve us ill."

Sorn walks over to the puddle-shaped etching on the floor of the cavern, kneels down, and inspects it more closely. Looking satisfied, he returns to the others.

"Something about this place feels right to me. And I'm not just talking about how defensible a position this is. It feels right. Safe and somehow familiar.

"As much as I'd like to stay here, I think that we should leave this place for now and ask Zacxh to seal it back up as best he can."
